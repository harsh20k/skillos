# Side Hustle: From Idea to Income in 27 Days (Action Plan)

Copy this file into Obsidian/Notes and tick the boxes daily. Keep scope small: one offer, one audience, one channel.

## 27-Day Checklist (Actionable Items)

### Day 1 — Pick a tiny, winnable lane
- [ ] Write 3 skills you already have (work + hobbies)
- [ ] Write 3 problems you can solve for others
- [ ] Pick **one** lane that overlaps (skill + problem + people you can reach)
- [ ] Define your success metric for Day 27 (e.g., “1 paying client” or “$100 revenue”)
- [ ] Create a simple name for the hustle (working title is fine)

### Day 2 — Define a clear customer
- [ ] Choose one customer type (e.g., “busy small business owners”, “new immigrants in Halifax”, “Dal students”)
- [ ] Write a 1-line customer description: *“I help ___ who ___.”*
- [ ] List where they hang out (2 online + 1 offline)
- [ ] Write 10 example people/companies you could message this week

### Day 3 — Problem interviews (no selling yet)
- [ ] Draft 6 interview questions (pain, current solution, budget, urgency)
- [ ] Message 10 people asking for a **10-minute** chat
- [ ] Schedule at least 2 calls/chat interviews
- [ ] Create a notes doc titled “Customer Insights”

### Day 4 — Confirm willingness to pay
- [ ] Conduct 2–3 interviews (call or chat)
- [ ] Capture exact phrases they use (copy-paste)
- [ ] Ask: “If this were solved, what would that be worth per month/once?”
- [ ] Identify the top 1 pain that appears repeatedly
- [ ] Decide: continue, pivot the audience, or pivot the offer

### Day 5 — Choose your simplest paid offer
- [ ] Pick **one** offer type:
  - [ ] Productized service (fixed scope, fixed price)
  - [ ] Coaching/consulting session
  - [ ] Digital product (template, checklist, mini-guide)
- [ ] Write your offer in one sentence: *“I will deliver ___ in ___ days.”*
- [ ] Define what is **in scope** (3 bullets) and **out of scope** (3 bullets)
- [ ] Choose a realistic first price (start low, not free)

### Day 6 — Create a “Minimum Viable Offer” page
- [ ] Write an offer page (Google Doc / Notion / simple markdown) with:
  - [ ] Who it’s for
  - [ ] The promised outcome
  - [ ] What they get (3–5 bullets)
  - [ ] Price + how to pay (or “Pay after onboarding call”)
  - [ ] A call-to-action: “DM me ‘START’” / booking link
- [ ] Add 1 short credibility line (experience, portfolio, prior results)
- [ ] Save a shareable link

### Day 7 — Set up payment + delivery basics
- [ ] Choose payment method (Stripe, PayPal, Interac e-Transfer, etc.)
- [ ] Create a simple intake form (Google Form) with 8–12 questions
- [ ] Create a delivery folder/template (what you will hand over)
- [ ] Draft a short “welcome message” for new clients

### Day 8 — Build the first version fast
- [ ] Create a v1 deliverable (template, report format, checklist, sample output)
- [ ] Time yourself: ensure you can deliver in your promised timeframe
- [ ] List 5 “quality checks” you will always do

### Day 9 — Proof: a tiny portfolio artifact
- [ ] Create one example/case study (even self-made):
  - [ ] Before/after
  - [ ] What you did
  - [ ] Outcome/metric (or expected outcome)
- [ ] Put it on a one-page portfolio (Notion/Drive link)
- [ ] Save 2 screenshots you can share quickly

### Day 10 — Write your outreach script (short + specific)
- [ ] Write 2 scripts:
  - [ ] Script A: cold message (60–90 words)
  - [ ] Script B: warm message (to people who know you)
- [ ] Include:
  - [ ] Who you help
  - [ ] The outcome
  - [ ] A question (“Worth a 10-min chat?”)
- [ ] Prepare 10 personalized openers (1 sentence each)

### Day 11 — Outreach sprint #1
- [ ] Send 10 messages (mix warm + cold)
- [ ] Post 1 short social post about the problem you solve (no hard selling)
- [ ] Track responses in a tiny CRM (sheet or notes):
  - [ ] Name
  - [ ] Date contacted
  - [ ] Status
  - [ ] Next follow-up date

### Day 12 — Run 2 discovery calls
- [ ] Hold 1–2 calls/chats
- [ ] Use this structure:
  - [ ] Problem
  - [ ] Impact
  - [ ] Current solution
  - [ ] Desired outcome
  - [ ] Offer + price
  - [ ] Next step
- [ ] Ask for permission to follow up in 48 hours

### Day 13 — Follow-ups that don’t feel pushy
- [ ] Follow up with everyone from Day 11–12
- [ ] Offer a choice:
  - [ ] “Want option A (quick) or option B (more thorough)?”
- [ ] If no response, schedule one more follow-up in 3–4 days

### Day 14 — Close your first sale (even small)
- [ ] Make a simple “starter deal” version (smaller scope)
- [ ] Send 3 direct offers to the most interested leads
- [ ] Try to collect:
  - [ ] 1 paid booking **or**
  - [ ] 1 commitment to pay after onboarding call
- [ ] If you get a “no,” ask: “What would have made this a yes?”

### Day 15 — Deliver like a pro (Day 1 of fulfillment)
- [ ] Confirm requirements via intake form
- [ ] Send a short timeline + what you need from them
- [ ] Produce first draft/output
- [ ] Share progress update (even if small)

### Day 16 — Deliver like a pro (Day 2 of fulfillment)

### Day 17 — Delivery + feedback

- [ ] Ask 3 feedback questions:
  - [ ] What’s most useful?
  - [ ] What’s missing?
  - [ ] Would you recommend this to someone?
- [ ] Ask for a testimonial (2–3 sentences)

### Day 18 — Turn delivery into repeatability

- [ ] Save reusable templates (copy/paste blocks)
- [ ] Identify 1 step to automate or simplify

### Day 19 — Add a simple guarantee or risk-reversal
- [ ] Choose one:
  - [ ] “1 free revision”
  - [ ] “Money-back if not delivered on time”
  - [ ] “Pay only if you’re satisfied”

### Day 20 — Outreach sprint #2 (bigger)
- [ ] Send 15 new messages

- [ ] Post 1 proof post (example + what changed)

### Day 21 — Improve your offer based on reality
- [ ] Adjust scope to match delivery time
- [ ] Adjust pricing if demand is strong or delivery is heavy
- [ ] Create 2 tiers (Basic / Premium)

### Day 22 — Create a mini “lead magnet” (optional but effective)
- [ ] Make one small free resource (1 page):
  - [ ] Checklist / template / audit scorecard

- [ ] Share it in 2 places (LinkedIn + a group/community)

### Day 23 — Build a tiny funnel (simple)

  - [ ] DM keyword

### Day 24 — Partnership or referral angle

## Explanations (Read After Doing the Tasks)

### Why “one audience + one offer” wins
Doing many things feels productive but delays revenue. A narrow offer reduces decision fatigue for you and clarity for buyers. You can broaden later once you have proof.

### What “Minimum Viable Offer” means
It’s the smallest version of a paid solution that:
1) produces a real outcome,  
2) can be delivered reliably,  
3) is easy to explain in one sentence.

### Why interviews before building
Interviews prevent you from building the wrong thing. You’re looking for:
- a painful problem,
- urgency (“I need this now”),
- and willingness to pay (budget or trade-off language).

### Pricing (simple approach)
Start with a price that feels slightly uncomfortable but not scary. If you close quickly, price is likely too low. If you never close, either price is too high for the perceived value or the offer isn’t specific enough.

### The “proof loop”
You earn trust fastest by showing:
- a clear example,
- a small result,
- and a repeatable process.
Each delivery becomes marketing material (case study, testimonial, screenshot).

### Outreach without spam
Personalized messages work because they show you understand the person’s context. Keep messages short and ask a low-friction question (10-minute chat). Follow-ups are normal; most people miss the first message.

### Systemization is the real side-hustle unlock
Your income grows when delivery becomes repeatable. Templates, checklists, and a weekly routine reduce mental load and make progress consistent.

## Quick Templates (Optional)

### 1-line offer
“I help **[audience]** achieve **[outcome]** in **[timeframe]** using **[method]**.”

### Short outreach message
“Hi [Name] — noticed [personalized detail]. I help [audience] with [outcome]. If you’re dealing with [pain], I can share a quick approach. Worth a 10-min chat this week?”

### Testimonial request
“If you found this helpful, could you write 2–3 sentences on what changed for you and who you’d recommend it to?”
