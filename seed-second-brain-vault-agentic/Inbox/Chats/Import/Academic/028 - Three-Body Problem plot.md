## How you can use it for YOUR productivity  
Given your background (freelancer in animation/graphic-design, teaching programming, building a meditation-app, moving to Canada, developing content library, etc) you can apply Motion in several concrete ways.

Here are some use-cases and how to set them up:

### Use-case 1: Manage your freelance/contract workload  
- Create a “Project” for each client job (e.g., “Hand-drawn animation for client X”, “Promo video Y”).  
- Use the AI Project Manager role to auto-break the project into phases/tasks (storyboarding → drawing → revision → delivery).  
- Sync your calendar so that tasks get scheduled into your day based on your availability.  
- Use the task manager to prioritise urgent client jobs vs. ongoing personal development (e.g., your meditation-app, content library).  
- The AI can alert you when you have overlapping tasks or too many high-priority items scheduled.  
This will help you keep track of both client work **and** your side-projects.

### Use-case 2: Content library + teaching schedule  
- Use a “Project” for your Medium article series (e.g., “Meditation content library – publish 4 articles”).  
- Use AI Docs/Notes features to help you draft articles faster: e.g., ask the AI to help structure an article, then you edit.  
- Use calendar auto-scheduling to block time for writing, editing, promoting.  
- If you are teaching programming (Python, SQL, PowerBI), use tasks to plan your session preparation, student follow-ups, and resource creation.

### Use-case 3: Big-move preparation (Canada relocation + PR + partner support)  
- Create a “Project” called “Canada move + PR + partner support”.  
- Break it into subtasks: visa medical follow-up, partner’s paperwork, relocation logistics, settling in Halifax, job discussion with your manager, budget planning.  
- Use calendar blocks to schedule time each week for paperwork, partner-check-in, language learning (you also want to learn French), apartment/search etc.  
- Use the Notes/Wiki feature to track all documents, links, deadlines in one place.  
- The AI can remind you of upcoming deadlines and potential delays (e.g., visa medical slot, notice period conversation).  

### Use-case 4: Day-to-day time & focus management  
- Let the tool auto-schedule your day: given your tasks and meetings it optimises where to fit in “deep work” (e.g., animation drawing, coding in Swift) and “shallow work” (emails, admin).  
- Use the AI meeting notetaker/summary so after teaching or meetings you don’t lose time transcribing.  
- Use the spreadsheet/database feature (“Sheets & Databases” in Motion) to track recurring tasks like your habit of cleaning one spot in the bathroom daily — done and tracked for streaks.
