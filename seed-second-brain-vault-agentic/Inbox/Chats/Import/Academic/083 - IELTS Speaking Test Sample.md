# IELTS Speaking Test Sample

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Here’s another complete IELTS Academic Speaking Test sample to help you practice further. It’s structured into Parts 1, 2, and 3, as you would experience in the actual test.

---

### **Part 1: Introduction and Interview**
The examiner will ask you basic questions about yourself, your background, and your interests.

1. **What is your full name?**
2. **Where do you live?**
3. **Do you enjoy living in your hometown? Why or why not?**
4. **Let’s talk about food. What’s your favorite type of food?**
5. **Are there any foods that you dislike? Why?**
6. **Do you enjoy cooking? Why or why not?**
7. **Do you prefer to eat at home or eat out?**
8. **How often do you eat out?**

---

### **Part 2: Long Turn**

In this part, you will be given a topic card and have one minute to prepare. You’ll then speak for one to two minutes on the topic.

**Describe a recent goal you set for yourself.**

You should say:
- What the goal is
- Why you set this goal
- How you plan to achieve it
- And explain how you feel about this goal.

---

### **Part 3: Discussion**

In this section, you will discuss more abstract ideas related to the topic from Part 2.

---

**Topic: Setting and Achieving Goals**

1. **Do you think it’s important to set goals in life? Why or why not?**
2. **What types of goals do people usually set for themselves?**
3. **Why might some people find it difficult to achieve their goals?**
4. **How can friends or family support someone in achieving their goals?**
5. **Do you think goal-setting techniques differ for personal goals versus career goals?**
6. **Is it better to set long-term or short-term goals? Why?**

---

**Topic: Planning and Motivation**

1. **What are some common reasons why people lose motivation to achieve their goals?**
2. **Do you think modern technology makes it easier or harder to stay motivated? Why?**
3. **How important is it to have a plan when working toward a goal?**
4. **Are there any benefits to failing to reach a goal?**
5. **What role does discipline play in achieving long-term goals?**

---

### Practice Tips

For **Part 2**, try to speak naturally, giving reasons and details for each part of the topic card. For **Part 3**, focus on expressing your ideas clearly and logically. You don’t have to know everything, but you should try to explain your views and provide examples if possible. Practicing these types of questions can help you feel more confident and ready for a variety of topics.
