# Melano-TX OD Information

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes. **Melano-TX OD (tranexamic acid)** *is prescribed for certain skin-related conditions*, but **off-label and under dermatologist supervision**.

### Skin conditions where it is used
- **Melasma / hyperpigmentation**
  - Helps reduce UV-induced pigmentation by inhibiting melanocyte activation.
  - Often used **short-term** and in **low doses**.
- **Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH)**
- **Refractory pigmentation disorders** where topical treatment alone is insufficient.

### How it is used in dermatology
- **Oral tablets** (like Melano-TX OD) – selected patients only  
- **Topical tranexamic acid** creams/serums  
- **Intralesional / micro-needling assisted delivery** (in specialist clinics)

### Important cautions
- It is **not a first-line skin medication**.
- **Risk of blood clots** exists, especially in people with:
  - History of thrombosis
  - Smoking
  - Hormonal contraceptive use
  - Coagulation disorders
- Usually prescribed for **limited duration** (e.g., 8–12 weeks).

### Summary
- ✔️ Yes, used for **melasma and pigmentation**
- ⚠️ **Off-label**, not cosmetic or OTC
- 🩺 Must be prescribed and monitored by a **dermatologist**

If you want, you can tell me **which skin issue** it was prescribed for (melasma, dark patches, acne marks, etc.), and I can explain whether its use makes sense and what alternatives exist.
