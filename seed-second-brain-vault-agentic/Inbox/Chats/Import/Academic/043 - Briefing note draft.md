**Briefing Note: Graduate Application System Update**

Dr. McAllister outlined the university’s ongoing initiative to expand the **Slate system**, currently used for undergraduate admissions, to include **graduate applications**. The Registrar’s Office serves as the central hub for undergraduate admissions, ensuring that all decision-making occurs in one place. In contrast, graduate applications are distributed across various academic units—often requiring input or approval from researchers and faculty members during evaluation stages such as interviews or departmental reviews.

The key objective of this upgrade is to **centralize the graduate admissions process** within the Slate platform, creating a unified, streamlined system for both undergraduate and graduate programs. This transition will enhance efficiency, reduce fragmentation, and ensure consistent management of applications across all programs. The system rollout is planned for **Fall 2025** for undergraduate admissions and **July 2025** for graduate applications.  

It was noted that **past information will not be migrated** to the new platform. Clear communication, audience awareness, and concise presentation of information will be essential throughout this deployment to maintain focus and avoid distractions.
