# Student life image idea

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes — **it is completely okay not to put your grade on LinkedIn**. In fact, for many IT students and professionals, it’s often the **better choice**.

### When it’s perfectly fine (and common)
- You have **projects, internships, or skills** to show
- Your profile focuses on **what you’re building and learning**
- You’re early in your career and still shaping your direction
- Your GPA is good but not exceptional (e.g., ~3.2–3.5)

Most recruiters **do not expect GPA on LinkedIn**. They look for:
- Skills
- Projects
- Tools
- Problem-solving ability
- Communication

### When adding GPA can help
- You’re a **student or recent graduate**
- GPA is **very strong** (typically 3.6+/4 or top 10–15%)
- You’re applying to **academically selective roles**, scholarships, or research positions

Even then, it’s optional.

### What to do instead (recommended)
Use that space to show **signal**, not numbers:
- “Built a distributed DBMS as part of coursework”
- “Hands-on with SwiftUI, Flask, PostgreSQL”
- “Learning cloud-native design and data systems”

This tells a much better story than a grade alone.

### One-line rule of thumb
> **If your work and learning say more than your GPA, you don’t need to list it.**

You’re not hiding anything — you’re **prioritizing relevance**.
