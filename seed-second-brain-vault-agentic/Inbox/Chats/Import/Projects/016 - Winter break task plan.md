# Winter break task plan

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes. **Ageism in the workplace is explicitly prohibited in Canada** under both **federal** and **provincial** law.

---

## 1. Federal Law (Canada-wide)

### **Canadian Human Rights Act**
- Applies to **federally regulated employers**:
  - Banks
  - Telecom
  - Airlines
  - Federal government
- **Age is a protected ground**
- Employers **cannot** discriminate in:
  - Hiring
  - Promotion
  - Training
  - Termination
  - Pay and benefits

**Exception**:  
Mandatory retirement may be allowed **only** if age is a **bona fide occupational requirement (BFOR)** (very rare and strictly tested).

---

## 2. Provincial Laws (Most Employees Fall Here)

Each province has its own **Human Rights Code**.  
All include **age** as a protected characteristic.

### Examples

#### Ontario
- **Ontario Human Rights Code**
- Protects workers aged **18+**
- Prohibits age-based discrimination in:
  - Job ads (“young team”, “recent graduate”)
  - Interviews
  - Layoffs
  - Promotions

#### Nova Scotia (relevant to you)
- **Nova Scotia Human Rights Act**
- Age is protected
- Applies to:
  - Hiring
  - Employment terms
  - Dismissal

---

## 3. What Counts as Ageism (Illegal)

Examples that can violate the law:
- Rejecting candidates because they are “too old” or “too young”
- Asking age-related questions in interviews
- Preferencing “young, energetic” workers
- Denying training or growth due to age
- Forcing retirement without legal justification

---

## 4. What Is Still Allowed (Legal)

- Decisions based on **skills, performance, or qualifications**
- Entry-level roles requiring **limited experience**
- Age-based distinctions tied to:
  - Pension plans
  - Benefits (within legal limits)
- Legitimate safety-based requirements (rare)

---

## 5. Enforcement & Remedies

If age discrimination occurs, an employee can:
1. File a complaint with:
   - Provincial Human Rights Commission, or
   - Canadian Human Rights Commission (federal)
2. Remedies may include:
   - Compensation
   - Reinstatement
   - Policy changes
   - Mandatory training for employer
