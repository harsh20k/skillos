## Summary  
Yes—while hand-drawn animations are less common than polished vector motion, several iOS apps integrate sketchy, handcrafted effects into their UI and interactivity to create a distinct, playful feel. From interactive storybooks that let you “pet” line-drawn animals, to puzzle games driven entirely by your ink-like gestures, developers are leveraging hand-drawn assets and micro-animations to engage users in novel ways. Below are key examples spanning children’s apps, creative tools, games, and prototyping platforms.

---

## Notable iOS Apps with Hand-Drawn UI Animations

### 1. Petting Zoo  
Christoph Niemann’s **Petting Zoo** is an interactive picture-book app for iPhone and iPad where each animal is rendered as a clean line-drawing that springs to life under your touch. Swiping a bunny stretches it like rubber; dragging along an alligator’s teeth makes them bend aside like grass citeturn3view0.

### 2. Hogworld: Gnart’s Adventures  
Snowcastle Interactive’s **Hogworld** adapts traditional storybook illustrations into a digital narrative. Its hand-drawn art is meticulously reconstructed for touch-based interactivity, so tapping and swiping feels as if you’re moving ink on paper, preserving a richly textured, analogue charm citeturn4view0.

### 3. Florence  
**Florence**, by Mountains and published by Annapurna Interactive, is an award-winning interactive storybook with chapters animated in a delicate, sketch-like style. You “brush” the heroine’s teeth by scrubbing sideways, “color-in” puzzles by dragging and dropping, and watch comic-strip panels transition with the feel of hand-drawn cells citeturn6view0.

### 4. Loop  
Universal Everything’s **Loop** offers a minimalist animation canvas on iPad, featuring a deliberately “hand-scribbled” interface with just three color brushes and onion-skin playback. The raw, pencil-like UI humanizes the drawing process, making the controls themselves feel hand-crafted rather than digital widgets citeturn0news30.

### 5. Paper by FiftyThree (now WeTransfer)  
The sketching app **Paper** uses hand-drawn metaphors throughout its UI: tools appear as tactile journal inserts, strokes rewind with a touch gesture, and pages flip with subtle shadows resembling real paper. Its fluid, pencil-style feedback cues reward exploration in an analog-inspired space citeturn2search5.

### 6. Auryn Storybook Apps  
Auryn’s children’s apps like *The Little Mermaid* and *What Does My Teddy Bear Do All Day?* are built on a proprietary “Aurynisation” engine that simulates watercolor-and-oil textures. Illustrations look painterly, and touching characters brings them gently to life with hand-drawn animations citeturn9view0.

### 7. Blek  
In **Blek**, your own gestures become the gameplay. You draw a single free-form stroke that perpetually loops and bounces around the screen, interacting with colored dots. The hand-drawn, inky lines repeat like calligraphic animations, making your own doodles the core UI metaphor citeturn12view0.

## Broader Hand-Drawn UI Design Practices

### Sketchy Control Animations  
Some iOS designers add subtle, hand-drawn flair to common controls—e.g., animating a checkmark as if sketched in real time or having radio-button fills appear with a quick, pen-like stroke—enhancing tactile feedback in standard form elements citeturn0search2.

### Crafting Assets with Animation Tools  
Artists often use apps like Autodesk Sketchbook or Krita to compose frame-by-frame hand-drawn animations, then export them as sprite sequences or vector paths for in-app integration. These tools remain go-to options for creating custom, organic motion citeturn0search1.

### Prototyping from Hand-Drawn Sketches  
Platforms like **AppSeed** can ingest rough UI sketches—boxes, arrows, and icons drawn by hand—and convert them into interactive prototypes, preserving the sketchy aesthetic while enabling early user testing and iteration citeturn8news20.

## Conclusion  
Integrating hand-drawn animations into iOS UIs can foster a unique brand voice, enhance user delight, and bridge digital interactions with the warmth of analog illustration. Whether through storybook apps, games, creative tools, or even subtle control effects, the hand-crafted motion trend continues to inspire fresh, human-centered design on the App Store.
