# AIS in vessel monitoring

## What VMS is
VMS is a **satellite-based tracking system** used by governments and fisheries authorities to **monitor the location, speed, and movement of fishing vessels**—mainly for **regulation, legality, and safety**.

## How VMS works
- Each fishing vessel carries a **VMS transmitter (often called a VMS beacon)**.  
- It sends location reports at fixed intervals (e.g., every 15–60 minutes).  
- Position reports go through **satellite communication** to a monitoring center.  
- Authorities use this to ensure vessels are fishing **in legal waters** and following rules.

## What VMS data includes
- Vessel ID  
- GPS position  
- Timestamp  
- Course  
- Speed  
- Sometimes gear type or status (depending on country)

## AIS vs VMS (important difference)
| Feature | AIS | VMS |
|--------|-----|------|
| Purpose | Safety, collision avoidance, general tracking | Enforcement, compliance, fisheries management |
| Broadcast | Open/public | Encrypted/private to authorities |
| Frequency | Real-time/seconds | Periodic (15–60 min) |
| Range | VHF + satellite | Satellite only |
| Who uses it | All ships | Mostly commercial fishing vessels |

## Where VMS is used
- Fisheries management (legal fishing zones, quotas)  
- Monitoring illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing  
- Marine protected area enforcement  
- Search & rescue
