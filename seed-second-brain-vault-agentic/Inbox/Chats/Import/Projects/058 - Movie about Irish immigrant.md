Here’s how it works with **UPI (Unified Payments Interface)** once you’re in **Canada**:  

---

## 📌 1. Using Indian UPI Apps (PhonePe, Paytm, GPay India, etc.)
- **UPI is India-centric**: UPI is run by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India) and officially works only with Indian banks.  
- **If you keep your Indian SIM active** (with roaming or international SMS enabled), and your **Indian bank account remains linked**, you *can still receive money* in your Indian account.  
- The sender in India will just transfer as usual; the money goes to your Indian bank account.  
- You’ll be able to check balances and receive in India.  

⚠️ Catch:  
- Some UPI apps may not work reliably outside India without OTP/SMS (which requires active roaming).  
- If your Indian SIM is inactive or blocked, you may not receive OTPs needed for login or verification.  

---

## 📌 2. Special Case: **International UPI (UPI–NIPL Partnerships)**
- NPCI has tied up with certain countries (like UAE, Singapore, Mauritius, Sri Lanka, France) where UPI is expanding for **payments**, not yet for general cross-border transfers.  
- As of now, **Canada is NOT yet integrated** into UPI’s international network.  
- That means you can’t receive Canadian dollars directly into UPI.  

---

## 📌 3. How People Usually Handle This
- **Keep your Indian account** active: Friends/family in India can keep sending you INR via UPI → you access it using internet banking or UPI app.  
- **To use in Canada**: You’ll either withdraw via Indian debit card at ATMs (with conversion charges), or transfer INR → CAD using services like Wise, Remitly, or bank wire.  
- Many students/professionals keep a small Indian balance for EMIs, LIC premiums, etc. and shift living money via forex/GIC/Canadian banks.  

---

✅ **In short:**  
Yes, you *can* still receive money via UPI in Canada **as long as your Indian bank account and SIM remain active**. But you’ll receive the money in INR in your Indian account, not as CAD in your Canadian bank.
