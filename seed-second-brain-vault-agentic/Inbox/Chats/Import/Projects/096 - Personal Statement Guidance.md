# Personal Statement Guidance

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

The document provides guidance on writing a personal statement, letter of intent, or statement of purpose (SOP) for academic applications. It outlines the purpose of such a document, which is to market oneself by sharing unique experiences, skills, and resilience, complementing the rest of the application.

Key points include:

1. **Differentiation**: A personal statement is distinct because it is narrative, personal, and demonstrates how past experiences shaped the applicant’s skills and motivations.

2. **Tips and Tricks**:
   - Follow specified word/page limits.
   - Personalize the statement for each program.
   - Use specific examples to highlight skills and experiences.
   - Address gaps or struggles in education or career by showing growth and resilience.

3. **Preparation**:
   - Research the program thoroughly, including its values, courses, and faculty.
   - Understand any specific questions or prompts provided.

4. **Structure**:
   - Organize ideas using a thematic or chronological approach.
   - Divide the statement into clear sections, akin to an argumentative essay.

5. **Writing Process**:
   - Focus on showcasing your personal growth, career goals, and fit for the program.
   - Highlight how the program benefits you and how you contribute to it.

6. **Editing**:
   - Ensure clarity, conciseness, and correct grammar.
   - Avoid clichés and vague statements.
   - Seek feedback from trusted individuals.

7. **Examples**:
   - Use specific and tailored examples over general statements.
   - Highlight meaningful experiences and connect them to the program.

The document also emphasizes avoiding informal language, focusing on individuality, and demonstrating readiness for academic challenges.
