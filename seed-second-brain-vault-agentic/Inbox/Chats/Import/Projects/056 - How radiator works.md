# How radiator works

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

That’s an old-fashioned **cast iron radiator**, usually part of a central heating system. Here’s how it works:

### 🔧 How It Works
1. **Heat Source**  
   Somewhere in the building (often a basement) there’s a **boiler**.  
   - If it’s a **hot water system**, the boiler heats water and pumps it through pipes into the radiator.  
   - If it’s a **steam system**, the boiler produces steam that rises naturally through the pipes into the radiator.  

2. **Radiator Function**  
   - The hot water or steam flows through the hollow sections of the radiator.  
   - The large surface area of the cast iron transfers heat into the room by **radiation** (warming objects and people directly) and **convection** (heating the air).  

3. **Heat Distribution**  
   - In a hot water system, the cooled water returns to the boiler to be reheated.  
   - In a steam system, the steam condenses back to water and flows back to the boiler.  

### ⚙️ The Controls
- **Valve (left side in your photo):** Lets water or steam in; turning it can regulate or shut off heat.  
- **Pipe (right side in your photo):** The return line for water/condensate.  
- Sometimes there’s a **bleed valve** (a small screw or knob at the top side) — this lets out trapped air so the radiator can fill properly with hot water.  

### 🔥 What You’ll Notice
- It takes a bit of time to heat up, but once warm, it stays hot for a long time because cast iron retains heat well.  
- Steam radiators may make hissing or clanking sounds as they heat.  
- Hot water radiators run quieter and more steadily.
