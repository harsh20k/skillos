# iPhone screenshot location change

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

On iPhone, **you cannot change the system-wide default save location for screenshots**. By design, iOS always saves screenshots to the **Photos app → Screenshots album**.

However, there are **workarounds** depending on what you want.

---

## What is NOT possible (system limitation)
- You cannot set screenshots to auto-save directly to **Files**, **iCloud Drive**, or a custom folder.
- There is no setting in **Settings → Camera** or **Photos** to change this.

---

## Practical alternatives (what *is* possible)

### 1. Save a screenshot to Files (manual)
After taking a screenshot:
1. Tap the **thumbnail preview**
2. Tap **Share**
3. Choose **Save to Files**
4. Select any folder (iCloud Drive / On My iPhone)

This avoids saving it permanently in Photos.

---

### 2. Automatically move screenshots using Shortcuts (best workaround)
You can auto-move screenshots to a folder using the **Shortcuts** app.

**Steps:**
1. Open **Shortcuts**
2. Go to **Automation**
3. Tap **Create Personal Automation**
4. Choose **App** or **Photo** → **When a photo is taken**
5. Add action: **Save File**
6. Choose destination folder in **Files**
7. (Optional) Add **Delete Photo** to remove it from Photos
8. Turn off **Ask Before Running**

Result:
- Screenshot appears briefly in Photos
- Automatically moved to your chosen folder

This is the closest behavior to a “default location.”

---

### 3. Hide screenshots from your main photo library
If clutter is the problem:
- Photos app → **Albums → Screenshots**
- Screenshots are already separated from Camera photos
- You can also **Hide** them individually
