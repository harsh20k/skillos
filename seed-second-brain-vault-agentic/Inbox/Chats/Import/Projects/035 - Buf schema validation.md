**GIS (Geographic Information System)** is a technology used to **capture, store, analyze, and visualize data on maps**.

In simple terms:

**GIS = data + location.**

It helps answer questions like:
- “Where are the highest-risk flood zones?”  
- “Which areas have the most traffic?”  
- “Where should new public services be placed?”  

It combines:
- **maps**,  
- **databases**, and  
- **analytics**  

to help governments, companies, and researchers make location-based decisions.

Examples of use:
- Google Maps routing  
- Urban planning  
- Environmental monitoring  
- Supply chain optimization  
- Telecom network planning  

**Concisely:**  
**GIS is a system that analyzes and visualizes data tied to geographic locations, helping make decisions based on “where” things happen.**
