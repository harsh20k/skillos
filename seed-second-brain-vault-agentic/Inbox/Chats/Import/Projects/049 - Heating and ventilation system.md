Great question — and very relevant for Halifax!  

Here’s the breakdown 👇  

### 🏢 **How common it is**
- **Steam heat** is an **older heating system**, quite typical in **heritage or mid-20th-century apartment buildings** around Halifax (especially in peninsular areas like South End or North End).  
- Newer buildings (built after ~2010) almost never use steam heat. Instead, they use **electric baseboard heaters**, **heat pumps**, or **central HVAC systems**.

### ⚙️ **Steam heat characteristics**
- It’s **centralized** — one boiler heats the whole building.
- Each suite only receives heat passively through the wall vents.
- There’s usually **no thermostat control** inside individual units.
- It’s generally **quiet and reliable**, but **less energy-efficient** than modern systems.

### 🌬️ **The air exchanger part**
- That **air exchanger in your closet** is a **modern addition**.  
  Many older buildings retrofit them to meet updated air-quality standards (especially in condos or renovated rentals).  
  It ensures you still get **fresh, filtered air** — something old steam systems alone didn’t handle.

---

✅ **In short:**  
Your building likely has a **hybrid setup** — **old-style steam heating** combined with a **modern air-exchange system**.  
That’s *very common* in **renovated older buildings** in Halifax, especially those updated within the past 10–20 years.
