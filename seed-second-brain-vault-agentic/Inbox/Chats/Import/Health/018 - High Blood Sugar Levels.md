# High Blood Sugar Levels

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

The **glycemic index (GI)** of sugar depends on the type of sugar, but here’s a summary of the most common types:

### Common Sugars and Their GI:
1. **Table Sugar (Sucrose)**:
   - **GI**: ~65
   - Moderate GI, meaning it raises blood sugar relatively quickly.

2. **Glucose**:
   - **GI**: 100 (the reference value for GI)
   - High GI, as glucose is absorbed rapidly.

3. **Fructose**:
   - **GI**: ~15–25
   - Low GI, because it is metabolized in the liver before entering the bloodstream.

4. **Honey** (varies by type):
   - **GI**: ~35–85
   - Depends on the ratio of glucose to fructose.

5. **High-Fructose Corn Syrup** (common in processed foods):
   - **GI**: ~58–62
   - Moderate GI, similar to table sugar.

### Key Points:
- **Sucrose** (table sugar) is commonly used and has a moderate GI, so it can cause a significant spike in blood sugar, especially in larger amounts.
- Fructose has a lower GI but can still contribute to insulin resistance and other metabolic issues when consumed in excess.

### Impact on High Blood Sugar:
- If you have high blood sugar levels (like yours), it’s best to **limit sugar intake** or avoid it altogether.
- Instead, use **low-GI sweeteners** like **stevia** or **erythritol**, which have minimal effects on blood sugar.
