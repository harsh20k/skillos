# Normal BP 33 Male

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes, a **blood pressure of 106/71 mmHg** for a **33-year-old male** is considered **normal and healthy**.

### Breakdown:
- **Systolic (106 mmHg)**: This is the top number, representing the pressure in your arteries when your heart beats. A normal range is **90–120 mmHg**.
- **Diastolic (71 mmHg)**: This is the bottom number, representing the pressure when your heart is at rest between beats. A normal range is **60–80 mmHg**.

### Conclusion:
- Your **BP is within the optimal range** and does **not indicate low or high blood pressure**.
- If you **feel energetic, don't have dizziness, fatigue, or fainting**, then this is a **very healthy BP reading**.

If you have other symptoms or medical concerns, it’s good to consult a doctor, but based on BP alone — you’re in the safe zone.
