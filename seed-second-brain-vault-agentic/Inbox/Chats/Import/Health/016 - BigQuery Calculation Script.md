# BigQuery Calculation Script

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

The values provided—HbA1c of 6.8%, fasting blood sugar (FBS) of 118 mg/dL, and postprandial (post-food) blood sugar of 112 mg/dL—indicate potential **prediabetes** or **early-stage diabetes**. Let’s break this down:

### 1. **HbA1c: 6.8%**
   - The HbA1c test reflects the average blood sugar levels over the past 2-3 months.
   - **Normal**: Below 5.7%
   - **Prediabetes**: 5.7% to 6.4%
   - **Diabetes**: 6.5% or higher

   An HbA1c of 6.8% is above the diabetes threshold, suggesting that the patient might have diabetes, even if blood sugar readings appear slightly inconsistent.

---

### 2. **Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS): 118 mg/dL**
   - FBS measures blood sugar levels after at least 8 hours of fasting.
   - **Normal**: Less than 100 mg/dL
   - **Prediabetes**: 100–125 mg/dL
   - **Diabetes**: 126 mg/dL or higher

   A fasting blood sugar of 118 mg/dL falls in the **prediabetes range**.

---

### 3. **Postprandial Blood Sugar (PPBS): 112 mg/dL**
   - PPBS measures blood sugar levels 2 hours after eating.
   - **Normal**: Less than 140 mg/dL
   - **Prediabetes**: 140–199 mg/dL
   - **Diabetes**: 200 mg/dL or higher

   A postprandial reading of 112 mg/dL is in the **normal range**.

---

### Interpretation
- **Mixed Indicators**: While the fasting blood sugar is in the prediabetes range and HbA1c indicates diabetes, the postprandial reading is normal. This may suggest:
  - **Early-stage diabetes**, where the body struggles to maintain fasting glucose but handles postprandial levels relatively well.
  - Possible **mismanagement of fasting glucose** due to diet, stress, or other factors.

---

### Recommendation
1. **Consult a Healthcare Provider**: A doctor may confirm the diagnosis with additional tests like an oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) or repeat HbA1c.
2. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - Adopt a balanced, low-glycemic-index diet.
   - Increase physical activity (e.g., 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily).
   - Maintain a healthy weight.
3. **Monitoring**:
   - Regularly monitor blood sugar levels.
   - Recheck HbA1c in 3 months to evaluate trends.
4. **Medication**: If confirmed as diabetes, the doctor might prescribe medications like metformin.
