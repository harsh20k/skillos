### Here’s how each could play a role:

#### **1. Metformin:**
- Usually does **not cause low blood pressure directly**.
- But if she’s **not eating well**, it can cause **weakness, low energy**, and **dizziness**, especially in older adults.
- Can contribute to **low blood sugar** (though rarely on its own), which may feel similar to low BP symptoms.

#### **2. Sitagliptin:**
- Also **does not directly lower blood pressure**, but when used with other medications (like insulin or sulfonylureas), it can increase the risk of **low blood sugar**.
- Low blood sugar can **mimic low BP** with symptoms like:
  - Sweating
  - Dizziness
  - Fatigue
  - Palpitations
