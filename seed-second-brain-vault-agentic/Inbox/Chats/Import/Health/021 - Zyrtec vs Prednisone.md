# Zyrtec vs Prednisone

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

**Does Odimont Also Contain Cetirizine?**

---

### **Overview**

**Odimont** is a brand name medication that contains **montelukast sodium** as its active ingredient. Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist used primarily for managing asthma and relieving symptoms of allergic rhinitis (hay fever). The standard Odimont formulation does **not** contain cetirizine.

However, there are combination products available under similar brand names that include both montelukast and **levocetirizine**, which is a close relative of cetirizine. These combination medications are designed to provide enhanced relief from allergy symptoms by targeting different pathways involved in the allergic response.

---

### **Details**

#### **Odimont**

- **Active Ingredient:** Montelukast sodium.
- **Uses:**
  - **Asthma Management:** Helps prevent asthma attacks and exercise-induced bronchoconstriction.
  - **Allergic Rhinitis Relief:** Reduces symptoms like sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, and itching.
- **Does Not Contain:** Cetirizine or levocetirizine.

#### **Odimont-LC (or Odimont Plus)**

- **Active Ingredients:**
  - **Montelukast Sodium:** As above.
  - **Levocetirizine Dihydrochloride:** An antihistamine similar to cetirizine.
- **Uses:**
  - Provides combined relief from both respiratory and nasal allergy symptoms.
  - Targets leukotrienes and histamines to reduce inflammation and allergic reactions.
- **Levocetirizine vs. Cetirizine:**
  - **Levocetirizine** is the active enantiomer of cetirizine, meaning it is a mirror-image version that can be more potent in smaller doses.
  - Both medications are used to relieve allergy symptoms but may have different dosing and side effect profiles.

---

### **Conclusion**

The standard **Odimont** tablet does **not** contain cetirizine; it contains only **montelukast sodium**. If you are looking for a medication that combines montelukast with an antihistamine similar to cetirizine, **Odimont-LC** (or a similarly named variant) contains both montelukast and **levocetirizine**.

---

### **Recommendation**

- **Consult Your Healthcare Provider:**
  - Before making any changes to your medication regimen, it's important to speak with your doctor or pharmacist. They can provide guidance on whether a combination medication like Odimont-LC is appropriate for your specific condition.
  - They can also inform you about the correct dosage and potential side effects based on your medical history.
