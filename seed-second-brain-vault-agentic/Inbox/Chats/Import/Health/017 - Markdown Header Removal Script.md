The **glycemic index (GI)** of **bhuna chana** (roasted chickpeas) is relatively **low**, typically around **30 to 35**. This makes it an excellent snack option for individuals looking to manage blood sugar levels, including those with diabetes or those on a low-GI diet.

---

### **Why Bhuna Chana Has a Low GI:**
1. **High Fiber Content:**
   - Roasted chickpeas are rich in dietary fiber, which slows down the absorption of carbohydrates, leading to a gradual rise in blood sugar levels.
   
2. **Complex Carbohydrates:**
   - The carbohydrates in chickpeas are complex, taking longer to digest and release glucose into the bloodstream.

3. **Protein-Rich:**
   - The high protein content further reduces the glycemic response, as protein slows down digestion.

4. **Minimal Processing:**
   - Roasting does not significantly alter the GI, unlike frying or adding sweeteners.

---

### **Nutritional Benefits of Bhuna Chana:**
- **Rich in Fiber:** Helps with digestion and blood sugar regulation.
- **High in Protein:** Supports muscle health and keeps you feeling full longer.
- **Low in Calories:** A great snack for weight management.
- **Rich in Micronutrients:** Contains iron, magnesium, and B vitamins.

---

### **For Diabetics and Low-GI Diets:**
- Bhuna chana is a **diabetic-friendly snack** because of its low GI and ability to maintain stable blood sugar levels.
- It is ideal for consumption as a standalone snack or paired with nuts for additional health benefits.

---

### **Portion Control:**
While bhuna chana has a low GI, consuming large quantities can still add to your carbohydrate intake. A standard serving size (about 30g or 1 small handful) is recommended for optimal benefits.
