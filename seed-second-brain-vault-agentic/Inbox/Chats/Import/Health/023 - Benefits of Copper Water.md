# Benefits of Copper Water

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Drinking water stored in a copper utensil, an ancient practice known as "Tamra Jal" in Ayurvedic medicine, is believed to offer several health benefits:

1. **Antimicrobial Properties**: Copper has natural antimicrobial properties, which can help kill harmful bacteria, fungi, and microorganisms in the water. This can reduce the risk of waterborne diseases.

2. **Improved Digestion**: Copper is known to stimulate the contraction of the stomach muscles, aiding in digestion. It also helps detoxify the stomach, regulate liver function, and ensure the proper elimination of waste.

3. **Anti-inflammatory Effects**: Copper has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the body, potentially benefiting those with conditions like arthritis.

4. **Enhances Immunity**: Copper is vital for the production of new cells and strengthens the immune system by helping in the absorption of iron, which is crucial for the body to fight off infections.

5. **Balances pH Levels**: Drinking water from a copper vessel can help balance the body's pH levels, making it more alkaline. This is believed to reduce acidity, improve metabolic function, and maintain overall health.

6. **Anti-aging Properties**: Copper is known for its anti-aging benefits. It helps reduce fine lines, wrinkles, and age spots by promoting the production of collagen and elastin.

7. **Brain Health**: Copper aids in the synthesis of phospholipids, which are essential for forming myelin sheaths that surround nerve cells. This can improve brain function and reduce the risk of neurodegenerative diseases.

It is important to note that while drinking water from a copper vessel can have benefits, overconsumption of copper can lead to copper toxicity. Therefore, it is recommended to use copper utensils for water storage occasionally, rather than continuously.
