The phenomenon is commonly described as **the Feynman Technique in action**, but the underlying idea is:

# **“You need deep understanding to explain something simply.”**

The name that best captures this is:

## **Feynman Effect / Feynman Technique Principle**
Explaining a concept to a child forces you to understand it so well that you can reduce it to its essential, clear parts. Richard Feynman emphasized that **simplicity requires mastery**.

Related concept:

## **Expertise Required for Simplification**
A well-known saying associated with this idea:  
> *If you can’t explain it to a 6-year-old, you don’t understand it well enough.*

While this isn’t an official cognitive bias, the closest formal term is:

## **The Curse of Knowledge**
Experts struggle to simplify because they forget what it’s like not to know something.  
Overcoming this requires deeper understanding and deliberate simplification—exactly what explaining to a child forces you to do.
