## ❄️ Why You Feel Colder
1. **Body Acclimatization**  
   - You just moved from India’s warm, humid climate.  
   - Your body hasn’t adjusted yet to Nova Scotia’s cooler, windier weather.  
   - It usually takes **a few weeks to a few months** for your internal thermostat to adapt.  

2. **Baseline Clothing Habits**  
   - Locals are used to cooler temperatures year-round.  
   - For them, +18°C feels “warm,” so shorts and T-shirts feel fine.  
   - For you, after Delhi’s +30–40°C summers, +18°C feels chilly.  

3. **Wind Chill Effect in Halifax**  
   - Halifax is coastal → **ocean winds make it feel 2–5°C colder** than the thermometer shows.  
   - Even when it’s mild on paper, the wind cuts through lighter clothes.  

4. **Metabolic & Lifestyle Differences**  
   - Locals often grow up in this climate → their metabolism and circulation adapt over years.  
   - They also wear layered clothing in winter, so when a mild day comes, they enjoy lighter clothes.  

---

## ✅ What You Can Do
- **Layer Up Smartly**: Instead of a heavy jacket, use T-shirt + light sweater/hoodie + windbreaker.  
- **Cover Extremities**: Thin gloves, socks, and a cap can make a big difference.  
- **Stay Active**: Walking, light exercise, or even hot drinks help raise body heat.  
- **Give It Time**: Your tolerance will improve in a month or two. By next summer, +15°C might feel comfortable to you too.  

---

💡 **Perspective**: Locals in Halifax wear shorts in 18°C the same way Delhi folks switch to sweaters when it drops below 20°C. It’s all about what your body thinks is “normal.”
