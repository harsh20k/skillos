# Dispersal Distance: Ecology Term

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

The dispersal distance of domestic cats (Felis catus) varies based on several factors, including whether they are feral or domestic. On average, domestic cats have a dispersal distance of about 1 to 3 kilometers from their original home territory. Feral cats, particularly those in less densely populated areas, can have larger territories and may disperse over distances up to 5 kilometers. These distances reflect the cats' need to find food, mates, and suitable shelter【24†source】.
