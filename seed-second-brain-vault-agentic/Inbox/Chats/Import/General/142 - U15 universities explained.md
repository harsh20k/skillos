###  History & Timeline of the U15 Group

- The **association** of Canada's research-intensive universities began in the **mid-1980s** with five Ontario-based institutions forming an informal group to advance their research agenda. citeturn0search1  
- In **1989**, additional Canadian universities joined, forming the **Group of Ten** in **1991**. citeturn0search1  
- The group expanded to the **Group of Thirteen** in **2006**, with the addition of Dalhousie University, the University of Calgary, and the University of Ottawa. citeturn0search1  
- Finally, in **2011**, the **University of Manitoba** and **University of Saskatchewan** joined, bringing membership to **15**. It was then renamed the **U15 Group**, and in **2012** a formal **U15 Canada Directorate** was created. citeturn0search1turn0search0  

Therefore, the **current list of 15 universities** has been **in place since 2011**, with the formal name “U15” adopted and institutional governance solidified in **2012**.
