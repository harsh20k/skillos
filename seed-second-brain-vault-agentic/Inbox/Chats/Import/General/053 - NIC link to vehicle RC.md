**C-DAC OTPs are not automatically used to take loans on your behalf.**

But here is the clear and safe explanation:

### 1. **OTP alone cannot be used to take a loan**
To take a loan in your name, a fraudster usually needs:
- Your **PAN**
- Your **Aadhaar**
- Your **mobile number**
- Access to **your OTP** (the most important part)
- Sometimes a video KYC

**Without the OTP, they cannot complete loan verification.**

### 2. **C-DAC OTPs by themselves are not dangerous**
C-DAC is simply a government SMS gateway.  
It only *delivers* OTP messages for govt services (Aadhaar, Parivahan, DigiLocker, etc.).  
C-DAC does not give loans, and OTPs from C-DAC do not indicate any financial activity.

### 3. **When does it become risky?**
It becomes risky **only if:**
- You shared the OTP with someone (phone call, WhatsApp, etc.)
- You entered the OTP on a **fake/phishing website** pretending to be a government portal

If you did not share an OTP or type it anywhere suspicious, **you are safe**.

### 4. Signs someone is trying to misuse your identity
You should worry only if:
- You get OTPs *without* trying to log in
- Someone calls you asking for an OTP
- Your credit score suddenly drops
- You get loan-related SMS you didn’t apply for

### 5. If you are unsure, you can check your credit activity
Check your CIBIL or Experian report for free.  
If no new loan is listed, then **no one used your identity**.
