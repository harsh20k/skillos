## 📌 When does an Indian become an **NRI**?

India’s definition is based on **days of stay in India**, not your visa type.  

### According to Indian Income Tax Act:
You are considered **Resident in India** if:  
- You stay **≥182 days** in India in a financial year (Apr–Mar), **OR**  
- You stay **≥60 days** in a year **AND ≥365 days** in the last 4 years.  

👉 If neither applies → you are **Non-Resident Indian (NRI)** for tax purposes.  

---

## 🎯 Example for You (2025–26):
- You’ll leave India in **Sep 2025** for Halifax.  
- Apr 1, 2025 – Aug 31, 2025 → ~150 days in India.  
- Since this is **<182 days**, you’ll qualify as **NRI for FY 2025–26** (Indian tax year).  
- From the year you leave, Indian income is taxed as NRI rules (higher TDS, limited deductions).  

---

## 🏦 Banking Implication
- Once you become NRI, you must:  
  - Convert resident savings a/c → **NRO** account.  
  - Open **NRE/FCNR** for foreign remittances.  
- Many people delay this, but legally you’re supposed to update bank status soon after changing residency.  

---

## 📑 Canadian Side
- CRA (Canada Revenue Agency) considers you a **tax resident of Canada** once you have “residential ties”:  
  - Living there full-time (rented/owned home).  
  - Spouse joins you.  
  - Job or studies start.  

👉 In your case: From **Fall 2025 (Sep)**, you’ll be treated as a Canadian tax resident.  
That year, you’ll be a **dual-status taxpayer**:  
- **Apr–Aug 2025**: Indian resident.  
- **Sep 2025–Mar 2026**: Canadian resident.  

---

✅ **Summary**:  
- You become **NRI for India** once you leave India (Sep 2025).  
- You become **Canadian tax resident** as soon as you settle in Halifax (Sep 2025).  
- From then, India will only tax income *sourced in India* (rent, dividends), while Canada will tax *global income*. Tax treaty avoids double taxation.
