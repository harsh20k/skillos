# P-value decision analysis

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Given the data, let's now calculate the ratios \( X \) and \( Y \) step by step.

### Country B:
- **Exported Goods in 2000** = 178 million
- **Exported Goods in 2010** = 108 million
- **Export Income in 2000** = 65 million dollars
- **Export Income in 2010** = 30 million dollars

**Change in Export Income for Country B**:
\[
\text{Change in Export Income (B)} = 30 - 65 = -35 \, \text{million dollars}
\]

**Change in Weight of Goods Exported for Country B**:
\[
\text{Change in Weight (B)} = 108 - 178 = -70 \, \text{million goods}
\]

Now, calculate \( X \):
\[
X = \frac{\text{Change in Export Income for B}}{\text{Change in Weight for B}} = \frac{-35}{-70} = 0.5
\]

---

### Country D:
- **Exported Goods in 2000** = 225 million
- **Exported Goods in 2010** = 200 million
- **Export Income in 2000** = 255 million dollars
- **Export Income in 2010** = 205 million dollars

**Change in Export Income for Country D**:
\[
\text{Change in Export Income (D)} = 205 - 255 = -50 \, \text{million dollars}
\]

**Change in Weight of Goods Exported for Country D**:
\[
\text{Change in Weight (D)} = 200 - 225 = -25 \, \text{million goods}
\]

Now, calculate \( Y \):
\[
Y = \frac{\text{Change in Export Income for D}}{\text{Change in Weight for D}} = \frac{-50}{-25} = 2
\]

---

### Ratio of \( X \) to \( Y \):
\[
\frac{X}{Y} = \frac{0.5}{2} = 0.25
\]

### Final Answer:
The ratio of \( X \) to \( Y \) is **0.25**.
