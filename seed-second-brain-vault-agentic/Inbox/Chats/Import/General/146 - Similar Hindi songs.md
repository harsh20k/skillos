Got it 👍 — you’re asking not about *musical similarity* (melody, tempo, style), but about **lyrical meaning and emotional resonance**.  
“**Yun Hi Chala Chal Rahi**” (*Swades*) is essentially about:  

- Journeying through life without overthinking  
- Embracing the unknown with curiosity and joy  
- Freedom, movement, and spiritual surrender  
- A philosophy of *“keep walking, keep flowing”*  

Here are **Hindi songs with similar meaning/emotion in lyrics**:

---

## 🌿 Songs About Journey, Carefree Living & Acceptance

- **“Musafir Hoon Yaaron”** (*Parichay, 1972*) – Kishore Kumar  
   *“I’m a traveler, I have no home, the road itself is my companion.”*  
   (Perhaps the closest lyrical cousin to *Yun Hi Chala Chal Rahi*.)

- **“Main Zindagi Ka Saath Nibhata Chala Gaya”** (*Hum Dono, 1961*) – Mohammad Rafi  
   *“I kept walking with life, never worrying about tomorrow.”*  
   (Philosophical, effortless acceptance of life’s flow.)

- **“Zindagi Ek Safar Hai Suhana”** (*Andaz, 1971*) – Kishore Kumar  
   *“Life is a beautiful journey, who knows what will happen tomorrow.”*  
   (Joyful embrace of impermanence.)

- **“Hai Apna Dil To Aawara”** (*Solva Saal, 1958*) – Hemant Kumar  
   *“My heart is a wanderer, it goes where it pleases.”*  
   (Carefree, light-hearted, like the mood of *Yun Hi Chala*.)

---

## 🌄 Songs About Freedom & Exploration

- **“Dil Chahta Hai”** (Title track, 2001)  
   *Celebrates friendships, journeys, and living in the moment.*  

- **“Safarnama”** (*Tamasha, 2015*) – Lucky Ali  
   *“Life is a journey, and the journey itself is the destination.”*  
   (Modern poetic take on the same theme.)  

- **“Phir Se Ud Chala”** (*Rockstar, 2011*) – Mohit Chauhan  
   *“I flew again… where the wind carried me.”*  
   (Metaphorical freedom and surrender to life.)  

- **“Ajeeb Dastan Hai Yeh”** (*Dil Apna Aur Preet Parai, 1960*) – Lata Mangeshkar  
   *About life’s strange, unpredictable path—accepting the mystery.*  

---

## 🌸 Spiritually Resonant / Reflective Songs

- **“Chala Jaata Hoon”** (*Mere Jeevan Saathi, 1972*) – Kishore Kumar  
   *“I keep walking, singing songs, wherever the road takes me.”*  

- **“Apni Marzi Se”** (*1920: Evil Returns, 2012*) – Kailash Kher  
   *About surrendering to life’s will, much like the spiritual undertone of Swades.*  

- **“Yeh Jo Des Hai Tera”** (*Swades, 2004*) – A.R. Rahman  
   *From the same film, reflective on belonging, roots, and inner journey.*
