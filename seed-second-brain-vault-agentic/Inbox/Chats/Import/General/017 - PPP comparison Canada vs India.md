## Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)

**Purchasing Power Parity** is an economic concept used to compare how much goods and services a unit of currency can buy in different places.  
Instead of using market exchange rates, PPP adjusts for **local cost of living**.

**Simple idea:**  
> If CAD 100 in Halifax buys the same basket of goods as INR 6,000 in Delhi, then those two amounts have equal purchasing power—even though the exchange rate may suggest otherwise.

PPP is useful for:
- Comparing **real living standards**
- Understanding **salary fairness across countries**
- Evaluating **cost-of-living differences**

## Comparison: Canada (Halifax) vs Delhi, India

### 1. Cost Structure (High-level)

| Category | Halifax, Canada | Delhi, India |
|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| Housing | Very expensive | Relatively cheap |
| Food (groceries) | Expensive | Cheap |
| Eating out | Expensive | Very cheap |
| Transport | Moderate–high | Low |
| Healthcare | Mostly public (tax-funded) | Mostly private, low cost |
| Services (maids, repairs) | Very expensive | Very cheap |

---

### 2. Purchasing Power Comparison (Practical View)

Assume **CAD 1 ≈ INR 60 (PPP-adjusted)**  
*(Market rate is ~INR 60–62, but PPP makes the comparison meaningful in real life)*

#### Monthly lifestyle equivalence

| Lifestyle | Halifax (CAD) | Delhi (INR) |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Basic single living | 2,000–2,500 | 60,000–75,000 |
| Comfortable middle-class | 3,000–3,500 | 90,000–110,000 |
| Very comfortable | 4,500+ | 140,000+ |

**Key insight:**  
INR 1 lakh/month in Delhi ≈ CAD 3,200–3,500/month in Halifax in terms of lifestyle.

---

### 3. Salary Perspective (PPP-adjusted)

| Nominal Salary | Real Meaning |
|--------------|-------------|
| CAD 60,000 in Halifax | Comparable lifestyle to INR 18–22 lakh/year in Delhi |
| INR 10 lakh in Delhi | Comparable to CAD 30–35k in Halifax |
| INR 25 lakh in Delhi | Comparable to CAD 70–80k in Halifax |

This explains why:
- Salaries look *much higher* in Canada
- But **savings potential** can feel lower
- And why many professionals in India live more comfortably on lower nominal income

---

### 4. Halifax vs Delhi — Reality Check

**Halifax advantages**
- Clean environment
- Strong public healthcare
- Better work–life balance
- Safer, less chaotic
- Long-term immigration stability

**Delhi advantages**
- Very high purchasing power for services
- Domestic help is affordable
- Eating out is cheap
- Faster lifestyle upgrades with income jumps

## Bottom Line

- **PPP explains why “high salary” ≠ “rich life”**
- Halifax salaries are higher, but **Delhi offers stronger purchasing power for daily life**
- Canada compensates with **social security, stability, and quality of life**
- India offers **cost efficiency and comfort** if income is strong
