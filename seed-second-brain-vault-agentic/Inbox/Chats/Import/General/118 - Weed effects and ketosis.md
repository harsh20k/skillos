# Weed effects and ketosis

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes, both **body fat** and **ketosis** can influence how cannabis affects someone, mainly because of how THC (the main psychoactive compound) behaves in the body.  

---

### 1. Body Fat and THC Storage
- **THC is fat-soluble**: It binds readily to fat (lipid) tissues.  
- People with **higher body fat** may store more THC in fat cells. This doesn’t always mean they feel “higher” longer during one session, but it can affect:
  - **Onset and duration**: Release can be slower, leading to slightly prolonged or muted effects.  
  - **Detection window**: THC stays in the body longer, so drug tests can show positive results for more days or weeks.  
- People with **lower body fat** may experience quicker onset, sharper peaks, but shorter overall duration.

---

### 2. Ketosis and Weed
- **In ketosis**, your body is burning fat for energy. Since THC is stored in fat, theoretically:
  - Mobilizing fat could release **stored THC back into the bloodstream** in small amounts.  
  - This could cause **unexpected light highs** or “flashbacks” in chronic users, though it’s usually subtle.  
- **Metabolic differences**: Ketosis shifts liver enzyme activity and may slightly change how THC and CBD are metabolized, but research is limited.  

---

### 3. Combined Effect
- Someone with **higher fat + ketosis** might notice a trickle release of THC, potentially extending effects in a less predictable way.  
- Someone with **very low body fat in ketosis** may feel stronger effects faster, since less THC gets buffered by fat storage.  

---

### 4. Other Considerations
- **Hydration and electrolytes**: Ketosis changes water balance; weed can worsen dehydration or dizziness.  
- **Blood sugar**: Both weed and ketosis can lower appetite and blood sugar, so combining the two can amplify light-headedness.  
- **Tolerance**: Long-term users with lots of stored THC may notice more fluctuation during fat loss phases (like ketogenic dieting or fasting).  

---

✅ In summary:  
- **Yes, body fat and ketosis make a difference.** More fat = more storage and longer release. Ketosis = burning fat may re-release THC, affecting how long or how unpredictably you feel it.  
- The effect is usually subtle unless you’re a **heavy, chronic user** or going through **rapid fat loss**.
