# Superconsciousness in Yogananda's Teachings

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Paramhansa Yogananda describes the state of superconsciousness as a heightened state of awareness where one transcends ordinary thought and identifies with the higher Self. In this state, the individual experiences a profound sense of unity with the divine, beyond the limits of the ego and physical reality. Yogananda emphasizes that superconsciousness is not an abstract concept but a direct, perceivable experience of joy, bliss, and intuitive knowledge. It is characterized by a deep sense of peace, expanded consciousness, and spiritual insight, where one sees the interconnectedness of all things and the presence of God in every aspect of existence.

In his teachings, he often explains that superconsciousness is a step above both the waking consciousness (ordinary awareness) and subconsciousness (the state of dreams and unexamined thoughts). He describes it as a state of spiritual clarity, where one feels liberated from the mental limitations and experiences a pure, uplifting connection with divine truth and love. Meditation is a primary means through which Yogananda recommends attaining this superconscious state.
