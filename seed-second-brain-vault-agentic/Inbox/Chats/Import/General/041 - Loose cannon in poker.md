# Loose cannon in poker

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

**Loose cannon** in poker refers to a player who:

- Plays **too many hands**  
- Bets and raises **aggressively and unpredictably**  
- Is **difficult to read** because their actions don’t follow standard logic  
- Can either **win big or lose big** due to this high-variance style  

### Why they’re called “loose cannon”
Because like a cannon rolling around unpredictably, they can **explode at any time**—sometimes in their favour, sometimes destroying their own stack or someone else’s.

### Typical traits
- Calls with weak or marginal cards  
- Bluffing in random spots  
- Overvaluing medium-strength hands  
- High risk-taking without solid strategy  

### Impact on the table
- **Volatile**: pots get bigger  
- **Dangerous**: solid players find it hard to predict them  
- **Profitable** long-term, because they make many mistakes
