# Keyboard battery carry-on rules

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

This section is asking whether you are carrying **any agricultural or food products** into Canada.  

Canada is very strict about these items — especially **meat, dairy, seeds, fruits, vegetables, wood, or animal products**.  

👉 You should answer:  
- **NO** → if you are not carrying any of these items. (This is the simplest and safest option. Most students tick *No* unless they absolutely need to declare something.)  
- **YES** → only if you are carrying food that falls into those categories (e.g. homemade snacks with meat, seeds, nuts, fruits, pickles, dairy products, plants). In that case, you must declare it and explain in “Additional details.”  

🔹 Even packaged items (like pickles, seeds, dried fruits, home food) can be restricted. If you bring such things, it’s better to declare “YES” and let the officer decide — undeclared food can lead to **fines up to CAD 1,300**.  

✅ For a smooth first arrival as a student, it’s best to **avoid bringing agricultural/food items** at all. Then you can safely select **NO** here.  

Do you want me to also give you a **list of safe food items** you *can* bring without issues (like sealed biscuits, chips, chocolates), so you don’t risk fines?
