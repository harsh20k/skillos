## 📄 How living-wage calculations are done

- The most recent calculation for Halifax (2025) puts the living wage at **≈ \$29.40/hr**. citeturn0search0turn0search1turn0search7  
- That rate is based on a “reference household” defined as **two working adults + two young children**. This reference includes costs for housing (3-bedroom), childcare, food, transportation, utilities, etc. citeturn0search1turn0search8  
- The idea is that *both* adults work full-time (35 hrs/week) to reach a household income that covers these expenses. citeturn0search1turn0search10

## ⚠️ Why using that number for a 2-person household may not work  

- The referenced living-wage rate assumes there are children — so a 2-person household (no kids) likely has different needs (no child-care, possibly smaller housing, lower food/transport costs).  
- Some “fixed costs” (rent, utilities, transport) remain similar, but other costs (childcare, larger apartment, child-related expenses) may drop — so the amount needed could be lower than the reference-case living wage.  
- However, because many costs are “fixed” (housing, utilities, transport), real savings for a 2-person household may not fully offset the drop in other costs — meaning they might still need a substantial hourly wage to live comfortably.

## 🎯 What a reasonable estimate could be for 2-person households  

Given that the published living wage is \$29.40/hr for a two-adult + two-child household:  

- For a 2-adult household *without children*, a reasonable living-wage target might be somewhat lower than \$29.40 — but likely **not drastically lower**, because housing and other fixed costs remain.  
- A rough ballpark might be **\$24-\$28/hr per adult**, depending on their rent, lifestyle, commuting, and whether both adults work full-time — but this is only an estimate (not from a formal report).
