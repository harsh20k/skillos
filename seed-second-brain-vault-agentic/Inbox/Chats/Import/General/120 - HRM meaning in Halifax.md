# HRM meaning in Halifax

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

In the context of **Halifax**, **HRM** stands for **Halifax Regional Municipality**.  

It’s the official municipal government area that was formed in **1996**, when the former cities of Halifax and Dartmouth, the town of Bedford, and Halifax County Municipality were amalgamated into one single regional government.  

A few key points:  

- **Geography**: HRM covers a very large area — over 5,000 km² — making it one of the biggest municipalities in Canada by land size. It includes urban, suburban, and rural communities.  
- **Population**: About **480,000+ people** live in HRM (as of 2025), making it the largest population center in Atlantic Canada.  
- **Governance**: It has a mayor and a council that govern all the former municipalities together, rather than separate city councils.  
- **Local usage**: When locals say “HRM,” they often mean the whole metro region, not just downtown Halifax. For example, someone in Dartmouth or Bedford still lives in HRM.  

👉 So in everyday life, “Halifax” usually means the city proper, while “HRM” refers to the wider municipality that includes Halifax *and* its surrounding communities.
