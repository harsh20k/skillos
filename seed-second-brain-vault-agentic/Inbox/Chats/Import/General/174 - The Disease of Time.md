### ⚖️ **Areas for Improvement**

**1. Structure and Flow:**  
While the rawness is a strength, it can make the piece feel scattered. The narrative jumps from one emotion or idea to another quickly — agitation, self-doubt, relationship dynamics, surrender, metaphysics.  
**Suggestion:** Consider light editing to group similar reflections together — e.g., worldly worry → self-image and relationships → desire to surrender → divine love and union.

**2. Grammar and Sentence Clarity:**  
Some sentences are run-on or unclear, which can interrupt the emotional flow. For example:
- “Its like taking a burden of me on my shoulders.” → “It’s like carrying the burden of *me* on my own shoulders.”
- “Its coming from a selfish place, if my relationship with the other person will affect.” → This could be clarified: “It’s coming from a place of fear — fear that I’ll lose the relationship if I’m not agreeable.”

A gentle grammatical polish (without removing the original voice) would make the piece more accessible to readers.

**3. Repetition of Ideas:**  
A few sentiments are repeated, which works well in devotional writing *if* the phrasing is varied. Here, some phrases could be trimmed or reworded to keep the reader engaged.
