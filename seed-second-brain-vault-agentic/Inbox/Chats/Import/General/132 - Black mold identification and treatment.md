# Black mold identification and treatment

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

Yes, what you’re seeing in the photo looks very much like **black mold (mildew)** growing on and under the caulking and seams of the bathtub/shower area.  

Here are some details:

### Why it looks like mold
- The dark black patches and streaks are typical of mold growth in damp areas.  
- It’s concentrated where water often sits (around seams and caulk lines).  
- The caulking has likely lost its seal, letting moisture collect behind it.  

### Health note
- Black mold can trigger **allergies, asthma, or respiratory irritation**.  
- Since this is in a bathroom where steam builds up, it can spread further if not treated.  

### What you can do
1. **Short-term cleaning**  
   - Wear gloves and a mask.  
   - Spray a mixture of **1 part bleach to 10 parts water** (or a store-bought mold/mildew cleaner) directly on the mold.  
   - Let it sit for 10–15 minutes, then scrub with a stiff brush.  
   - Rinse thoroughly.  
   *(If you prefer non-bleach: use vinegar or hydrogen peroxide.)*

2. **Long-term fix**  
   - The mold has penetrated the caulking, so even after cleaning, stains may remain.  
   - The best solution is to **remove the old caulk** completely, scrub/clean the area, let it dry fully, and then **re-caulk with mold-resistant bathroom caulk**.

3. **Prevention**  
   - Keep the bathroom well-ventilated (open a window or use an exhaust fan).  
   - Wipe down or squeegee shower walls after use.  
   - Regularly check for water leaks.  

👉 If you’re renting, this is something you should **document (with photos)** and inform the landlord about, since mold in living spaces is a health hazard and generally their responsibility to fix properly.
