# Conditional call forwarding fix

**Priority:** Medium

## Conversation

### Assistant

That message — **“Conditional Call Forwarding Active”** — is common on Samsung and other Android phones. Here’s what it means and why your calls are disconnecting:

---

## 📌 What the Message Means
- **Conditional call forwarding** is a network feature. It forwards calls automatically when certain conditions are met:
  - When you **don’t answer**  
  - When your phone is **busy**  
  - When your phone is **out of coverage** or **switched off**

Normally, you’ll just see this notification briefly, but it shouldn’t stop you from making calls.

---

## ❌ Why Your Call Disconnects
If your outgoing calls drop after this message, likely causes are:
1. **Carrier/network issue** – Sometimes the network’s call forwarding settings are misconfigured.  
2. **Incorrect call forwarding setup** – If you (or your carrier) set forwarding to a wrong or inactive number.  
3. **SIM card/line issue** – The network might not be registering your line correctly.  
4. **Software glitch** – Cached call settings on your phone can cause failures.  

---

## ✅ How to Fix It
Try these steps in order:

### 1. Check Call Forwarding Settings
On Samsung phones:
- Open **Phone app** → tap **⋮ (More options)** → **Settings** → **Supplementary services** (or **Call forwarding**).  
- Check if **Voice call forwarding** is ON (for "Always forward" or conditional options like "Forward when busy").  
- Turn them **OFF** to test.

### 2. Reset Network Settings
- Go to **Settings → General Management → Reset → Reset network settings**.  
- This clears network bugs but won’t delete personal data (only Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and APN settings reset).

### 3. Test with Another SIM
- Insert your SIM into another phone, or another SIM into your Samsung.  
- If the issue follows the SIM → it’s a **carrier problem**.  
- If it stays with the phone → it’s a **phone/software issue**.

### 4. Contact Your Carrier
Ask them to:
- Reset your **call forwarding settings** on their system.  
- Check if your line has any **restrictions** or **provisioning errors**.

### 5. Update or Restart
- Restart the phone after each change.  
- Check for **software updates** in Settings → Software update.
