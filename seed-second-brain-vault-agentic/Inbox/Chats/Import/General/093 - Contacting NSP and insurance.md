# Contacting NSP and insurance

**Priority:** Medium

### Assistant

Good question — and it’s **great that you’re checking this before your move-in date.**  

Here’s exactly what you can expect from **Nova Scotia Power (NSP)** 👇  

---

### ⚡ Typical Timeframe to Start Service

| Situation | Estimated Time |
|------------|----------------|
| **New tenant moving into an existing apartment (power already active in the unit)** | ⏱️ **Same day** or **within 1 business day** after you set up your account. |
| **If power is currently disconnected** | ⚙️ **1–3 business days** (they may need to send a technician to turn it back on). |
| **If it’s a new building or unit never had service** | 🏗️ **Up to 5 business days**, depending on scheduling and inspection. |

---

### 🧾 What You’ll Need When You Call / Apply Online
- Your **full address**: 21-6101 South Street, Halifax, NS B3H 1T2  
- **Start date** (usually your lease start or key pick-up date)  
- **Government ID**  
- **SIN (optional)** but helps verification  
- **Bank/credit info** for setting up pre-authorized payments or deposit  

---

### 💵 Security Deposit (if applicable)
NS Power may charge a **deposit (typically $150–300)** unless you:
- Provide a **good credit check** or  
- Get a **letter of reference** from a previous utility company  

Deposits can be waived if you’ve had good payment history elsewhere.

---

### ✅ Recommendation for You
Since Killam requires the *confirmation number* **on or before your key pick-up date**,  
👉 **set up your Nova Scotia Power account at least 3–5 days before** that date.  
That way you’ll get your confirmation number by email right away, even if activation happens later.
