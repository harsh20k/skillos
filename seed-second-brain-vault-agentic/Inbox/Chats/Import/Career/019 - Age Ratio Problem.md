To determine Alice's position relative to her starting point, let’s analyze her movements step by step on a coordinate plane:

---

### Step 1: Represent movements as coordinates  
1. Alice **starts at the origin**: \( (0, 0) \).  
2. Alice walks **7 km south**:  
   - She moves down the y-axis by 7 units.  
   - New position: \( (0, -7) \).  
3. Alice turns **right** and walks **4 km**:  
   - Turning right (facing west → east), she moves 4 units to the right along the x-axis.  
   - New position: \( (4, -7) \).  
4. Alice turns **left** and walks **5 km**:  
   - Turning left (now facing south), she moves 5 units further down along the y-axis.  
   - New position: \( (4, -12) \).

---

### Step 2: Find Alice’s displacement
Her final position is \( (4, -12) \), and the starting position is \( (0, 0) \).  
To find the displacement (shortest distance), we use the distance formula:

\[
d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}
\]

Substitute the coordinates \( (4, -12) \) and \( (0, 0) \):

\[
d = \sqrt{(4 - 0)^2 + (-12 - 0)^2}
\]
\[
d = \sqrt{4^2 + (-12)^2}
\]
\[
d = \sqrt{16 + 144} = \sqrt{160}
\]
\[
d \approx 12.65 \, \text{km}
\]

---

### Step 3: Direction relative to the starting point
- Alice’s final position \( (4, -12) \) indicates:
   - She is **4 km east** (positive x-direction).  
   - She is **12 km south** (negative y-direction).

This places her in the **south-east direction** relative to the starting point.

---

### Final Answer:
Alice is approximately **12.65 km away** in the **south-east direction** relative to her starting point.
