# 📋 Resume Review – Key Takeaways

### 1. **Education**
- Move your **education section to the top** (important for employers to see right away).  
- Don’t include **high school**; focus only on post-secondary and beyond.  
- For certifications:
  - Add the **date of completion** (helps show recency).  
  - Clarify if certifications **expire** or are **lifetime**.  

---

### 2. **Projects**
- Clearly **label projects** as:
  - **Course projects** (part of certification/degree)  
  - **Personal projects** (self-initiated, shows initiative & motivation)  
  - **Work-related projects** (freelance, professional, etc.)  
- Employers like seeing **personal initiative projects** (e.g., self-learning in evenings).  
- Include your **app projects** (animation data apps, etc.) — they show creativity and tech ability.  
- Keep a **master resume/work document** with all projects, then tailor each version for a job by selecting relevant ones.  

---

### 3. **Experience**
- Two years in **systems engineering/software** (Infosys).  
- Then **animation/design freelance work**.  
- Present animation/design background as:
  - Evidence of **creativity** and **problem-solving from a different perspective**.  
  - **Holistic approach** to tech problems (not just coding).  
- Adjust emphasis depending on job:
  - **Startups** → highlight versatility (tech + creative skills).  
  - **Large companies (IT helpdesk, corporate roles)** → focus more on the technical side; reduce emphasis on creative work.  

---

### 4. **Skills Section**
- Include both **technical** and **interpersonal skills**:
  - Technical → tailored to job description keywords (ensure ATS readability).  
  - Interpersonal → communication, time management, problem-solving, collaboration, etc.  
- Avoid **tables or text boxes** for skills → some ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) can’t read them properly. Use plain text or bullet points.  

---

### 5. **Formatting & Targeting**
- Resume should be **max 2 pages**.  
- Tailor each resume to the **job description**:
  - Pick the **most relevant bullet points** for that employer.  
  - Shrink or expand sections depending on the role (creative-heavy vs purely technical).  
- Marketing mindset → showcase what’s most important **to the employer**, not just everything you’ve done.  

---

### 6. **Extras**
- Include links to **GitHub and/or LinkedIn** (if active and up to date).  
- Employers (especially for co-ops/internships) may value **collaboration, learning ability, adaptability** even more than pure technical skills.  

---

### 7. **Next Steps**
- Build and maintain a **“master resume” document** with:
  - Every project, role, certification, volunteer experience, etc.  
  - Use this as a source to create **targeted versions** for each application.
