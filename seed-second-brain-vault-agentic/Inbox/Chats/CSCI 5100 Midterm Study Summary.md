# CSCI 5100 – Communicating Computer Science Concepts  
## Midterm Exam Study Summary (Fall 2025)

Professor: CA Heggie  
Program: MACS, Faculty of Computer Science, Dalhousie University  

---

## 1. Core Definition of Communication

**Communication** is:
- The sharing of information, ideas, thoughts, and emotions
- Interactive and meaning-driven
- Between one or many participants
- A process and exchange, not a one-way transfer
- Can be verbal, non-verbal, written, or visual

Key idea for exams:  
➡️ Communication is successful **only when understanding occurs**, not merely when something is said.

---

## 2. Communicating Computer Science Concepts

When explaining any CS concept, always address:

- **Who** – audience (CEO, peer, non-technical user, child)
- **Why** – purpose of communication
- **What** – core idea or concept
- **When / Where** – context
- **How** – language, structure, medium

Exam insight:  
Different audiences require **different abstractions**, not different facts.

---

## 3. Hearing vs Listening

- **Hearing**: Passive reception of words or signals  
- **Listening**: Active interpretation and understanding  

Signs of listening:
- Ability to paraphrase accurately
- Ability to respond appropriately
- Awareness of what is *not* being said

Miscommunication often occurs when people hear words but fail to listen to intent.

---

## 4. Miscommunication

**Miscommunication** = failure to communicate adequately.

Common causes:
- Misreading / mishearing
- Misinterpretation
- Language barriers
- Accent, dialect, cadence
- Multitasking and inattentiveness
- Focusing on response instead of message

Fallout:
- Loss of credibility
- Errors in decision-making
- Damaged professional relationships

Exam angle:  
Be prepared to **analyze why miscommunication occurred** and how it could be prevented.

---

## 5. Professionalism in Communication

Professional communication affects:
- First impressions
- Perceived credibility
- Trust
- Authority and expertise

Includes:
- Verbal and written clarity
- Body language (non-verbal)
- Cultural and societal norms
- Neutral, respectful tone

Professionalism is not optional in graduate or industry contexts.

---

## 6. Briefings & Advice

A **briefing** is the communication of advice, often from an SME.

Types:
- Written briefing note
- Oral briefing
- Follow-up or milestone briefing
- Informal or ad-hoc updates

Good briefings are:
- Brief and concise
- Factual
- Purpose-driven
- Understandable
- Accountable

---

## 7. Structure of a Briefing Note (Exam-Relevant)

**Topic** – One-line title  
**Issue** – 1–2 sentence problem statement  
**Background** – Key factual context (bulk of note)  
**Recommendations** – Short, actionable bullets  
**Next Steps** – Immediate actions  
**Metadata** – Author, date, version

Key rule:  
❌ No analysis or recommendations in the background section.

---

## 8. Writing as Communication

Writing is:
- Any composed text (emails, reports, documentation, notes)
- Always audience-dependent

Professional writing should be:
- Concise
- Factual
- Clear
- Credible
- Specific
- Correct

Avoid:
- Slang and colloquialisms
- Emotional arguments
- Over-explaining

---

## 9. Effective Communication When Writing

Four dimensions:
1. **Language** – plain, clear, proofread
2. **Frame of Reference** – audience + purpose
3. **Emotion** – neutral, fact-based
4. **Distraction** – focus, structure, white space

Exam tip:  
White space and layout affect understanding as much as wording.

---

## 10. Paraphrasing vs Précis

### Paraphrasing
- Rewrite in your own words
- Maintain original meaning
- Change structure and vocabulary
- Demonstrates understanding

### Précis
- Condensed summary
- Captures main argument and logic
- Retains tone and intent
- Removes detail, keeps essence

Likely exam task:  
➡️ Compare, paraphrase, or précis a given passage.

---

## 11. Plagiarism

Plagiarism includes:
- Copying without citation
- Presenting others’ work as your own
- Improper paraphrasing

Consequences:
- Grade penalties
- Course failure
- Program suspension or expulsion

---

## 12. Visual & Design Communication (Amuse-bouche)

Principles:
- **KISS** – Keep It Simple
- Fonts influence perception and bias
- Serif vs sans-serif matters
- Use bold/italics intentionally
- White (negative) space improves clarity

Proofreading tip:
- Change spacing or reading context to reveal errors

---

## 13. Exam Format Awareness

- Handwritten
- Short answers + long answers
- No MCQs or T/F
- Tasks include:
  - Paraphrasing
  - Précis
  - Analysis of communication scenarios

Key strategy:
Answer **clearly, directly, and with structure**. Avoid bullets in long answers unless explicitly helpful.

---

## 14. Core Takeaway for Midterm

Communication = **meaning + understanding + audience alignment**  

Most failures in CS communication are not technical — they are human.
