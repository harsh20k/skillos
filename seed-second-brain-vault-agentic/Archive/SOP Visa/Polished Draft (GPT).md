## STATEMENT OF PURPOSE TO STUDY IN CANADA

### Introduction

I, Harsh Pandey, have been accepted into the Master of Applied Computer Science (MACSc) graduate program at Dalhousie University, Nova Scotia. My ambition to pursue this program comes from my passion for technology, creative problem-solving, and my commitment to contribute to the current tech landscape in India.

### Academic and Technical Background

I was introduced to the world of computing at an early age through my fascination with programming and computers in general. I spent countless nights in high school in front of C++ coding screens, working on various personal projects. A key inspiration was my uncle, who worked at a renowned U.S.-based tech firm and encouraged me to take my passion seriously.

During my Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science and Engineering, I worked on several projects using Python, MATLAB, and C++. My final-year project focused on developing a solution for maintaining digital ownership integrity in photography. This hands-on experience enhanced my problem-solving abilities, strengthened my mathematical foundation, and taught me the value of collaboration.

Courses like Digital Electronics left a lasting impression on me. Understanding the logic systems behind computing deepened my curiosity. Outside the classroom, I pursued professional training in Advanced Java and web application development. One notable project was a custom application I developed for my hostel's LAN network, enabling student interaction and communication. An introductory course in Artificial Intelligence further inspired me to build a basic Ludo-playing bot using elementary machine learning principles.

### Professional and Creative Experience

Post-graduation, I joined Infosys as a Systems Engineer, working on large-scale software development and optimization projects. I collaborated with multiple teams, gaining hands-on experience in Java, modern web technologies (JavaScript, APIs), debugging, and managing complete software life cycles. My backend optimization work for an e-commerce client was recognized internally and helped solidify my technical foundation.

_**In 2015, driven by a long-standing creative streak and interest in visual storytelling,**_ I transitioned into freelancing with a focus on animation and design. I worked on a diverse range of projects in advertising, marketing, and the creative media industry, collaborating with talented artists and innovators. My portfolio includes hand-drawn animations, promotional campaigns, music videos, and interactive visuals. For example, I created a visual campaign for an eco-friendly brand and _**incorporated a basic machine learning model to generate word clouds**_ that enhanced visual storytelling in a music video.

While working creatively, I continued to explore technical tools like Adobe Suite, Blender, 3DS Max, and Maya. I often combined animation with interactive logic using programming principles. My drive to upskill led me to pursue courses in data science and visualization using SAS, R, Python, and JavaScript. I also learned Swift programming and developed iOS apps to organize and archive a growing library of over 300 animation projects. _**These experiences have kept me engaged at the intersection of design, data, and technology.**_

### Why Graduate Study Now

As I matured in this interdisciplinary space, _**I recognized the need to formalize my skills and gain a structured, global perspective on applied computer science.**_ I am particularly interested in data management, mobile computing, and user experience design—fields that are crucial in developing accessible, user-focused, data-driven technologies. A graduate degree will help me refine my technical abilities, strengthen my innovation mindset, and prepare me to contribute meaningfully to real-world challenges.

### Why Dalhousie University

Dalhousie University stood out with its unique blend of academic excellence, industry relevance, and experienced faculty. The MACSc program offers core courses like Advanced Cloud Architecting, Mobile Computing, Data Management, and Designing for User Experience—all of which align perfectly with my interest in building efficient and impactful digital solutions. I am particularly excited about Dalhousie’s association with DeepSense, an innovative research initiative that applies AI and ML to solve real-world challenges in the ocean economy. _**Opportunities like these—where applied science meets industry—are rare and immensely valuable.**_

Additionally, the program’s entrepreneurship stream will allow me to explore the business side of technology development and translate ideas into viable solutions. Together, these elements make Dalhousie an ideal environment for me to grow as a creative, technically-strong, and future-ready computing professional.

### Why Canada

 Canada represents an ideal destination for higher education due to its progressive research culture, global reputation in applied sciences, and inclusive, multicultural environment. Its strong academic infrastructure, post-study work opportunities, and emphasis on innovation offer international students like me the opportunity to grow both professionally and personally.   
 
 While I’m grateful for the education I received in India, I often felt that the learning was mostly focused on theory, with not enough emphasis on real-world application. There were few chances to work on hands-on or interdisciplinary projects, and many professors, though knowledgeable, didn’t always have direct experience with the latest tools or industry practices.
 
 I’ve also had close friends who studied and worked internationally, and hearing about their experiences has given me valuable insight into the differences in teaching styles, exposure, and career pathways abroad. Their journeys have reinforced my confidence in choosing Canada for my own academic growth, and I believe it will provide the kind of learning environment that helps me grow into a well-rounded, industry-ready professional.

### Future Plans and Return to India

After completing my program at Dalhousie University, I plan to return to India and apply my skills in roles such as Business Intelligence Analyst, Technology Consultant, or potentially as a tech entrepreneur. I aim to work on real-world challenges in healthcare, education, and creative media. For instance, _**I hope to contribute to the development of modular learning platforms that make personalized education more accessible or create low-cost diagnostic tools for underserved populations.**_

Inspired by my mother, who always emphasized the transformative power of education, I am deeply motivated to use technology for social good. This program will empower me to work on innovative, high-impact solutions that address India’s evolving needs.

_**I am also rooted here personally and financially.**_ My tuition for the first year (16,000 CAD) is already paid, and I have a GIC of 12,000 CAD along with secured funds for the next academic year. _**My family in India is financially secure with multiple income streams, including rental properties and ongoing businesses.**_ My mother, who runs a boutique business, is well-supported, and I look forward to returning home to support our family’s goals and contribute to India's dynamic tech industry.

### Conclusion

I am eager to join Dalhousie University's MACSc program and begin this next chapter in my professional journey. With a diverse background in software development, design, animation, and a strong passion for applied computing, _**I believe this program will help me evolve into a well-rounded, impact-driven professional.**_ I respectfully request the approval of my study permit to pursue this opportunity and fulfill my vision.
