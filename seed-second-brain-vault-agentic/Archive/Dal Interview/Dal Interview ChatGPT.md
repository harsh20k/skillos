Preparation Tips:

1. **Communication Skills:**

	- Practice explaining technical concepts to someone without a technical background. For example, explain a project you worked on or a problem you solved.
	- Use the STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your responses:
	- Situation: Briefly describe the context.
	- Task: Explain your goal.
	- Action: Detail the steps you took.
	- Result: Highlight the outcome.
	- Record yourself to check for clarity and coherence.

2. **Problem-Solving:**

	- Focus on explaining your thought process step by step.
	- Practice solving problems on platforms like LeetCode or HackerRank, but focus on articulating how you approach the problem.
	- Be prepared to handle algorithmic or logical puzzles.

3. **Technical Skills:**

	- Review foundational topics:
	- [[Algorithms and Data Structures]]: Practice [[Sorting Algorithms]], [[Search Algorithms|searching]], [[recursion]], and dynamic programming problems.
	- [[Programming Paradigm|Programming]]: Ensure you’re comfortable writing code in at least one of [[Java]], [[Python]], or C++.
	- [[SQL]]: Practice writing queries for relational databases, including [[Joins in SQL|joins]] and [[aggregate]] functions.
	- [[Operating Systems]]: Brush up on basic [[Unix commands]] and concepts like [[file systems]] and [[networking]].
	- [[Software Engineering]]: Review principles like version control ([[Git]]), [[software lifecycle]], and [[testing]].
	- Prepare to explain your [[past coding projects]], focusing on the design and challenges.

4. **General Tips:**

	- Go through the resources provided in the document, especially on the [[Summary and Takeaways STAR Interview Method|star framework]] and interview navigation.
	- Be prepared to demonstrate your skills via coding or [[Pseudocode]], and explain your logic as you go.
	- Review your application materials, like your [[SOP Dalhousie|SOP]], and align your responses with your stated goals and experiences.
	- Stay calm and approach each question systematically.

**[[Sample Questions]]:**

- Communication: “Explain a project where you had to solve a challenging problem. How did you approach it?”
- Problem-Solving: “How would you find the shortest path in a graph?”
- Programming: “Write a function in Python to check if a string is a palindrome.”
- Database: “Write an [[SQL]] query to find the second highest salary from a table.”

By preparing systematically and practicing your responses, you’ll build confidence for the interview. Let me know if you’d like to dive deeper into any specific topic or need help with mock questions!
