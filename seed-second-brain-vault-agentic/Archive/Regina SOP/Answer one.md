I am fascinated by the developments in technologies, like how my watch can detect my sleep cycle and how a Boston Dynamics robot can do a perfect summersault. The science of extracting valuable insights from data is growing at an unprecedented rate and being implemented by various industries. The potential impact I could make by helping people in better decision making driven by my passion for finding patterns and creative problem-solving, has motivated me to pursue this field of study. 

My interest started during my undergraduate studies, where I built a strong foundation in programming, problem-solving and maths. The project I built on a social network made me confident to solve problems with real significance. In the final year, the project on digital watermarking for the protection of rightful ownership was the turning point where I started focusing on algorithms as a means for a better future. This also fuelled my interest in media. 

My career experience also has strengthened my curiosity and passion for learning. At Infosys, I worked on optimising large software systems, which taught me how data can improve efficiency and reliability. Later, as a freelance animator, I used machine learning to create animated music videos based on music lyrics, which I never thought was possible, teaching me a unique lesson about what happens when creativity meets technology.

What truly motivates me is the chance to make a positive impact - whether it's helping businesses work more efficiently, improving medical diagnosis, or making learning easier for people everywhere. Data science and machine learning bring together the technical expertise, logical thinking and creative problem-solving that I find both challenging and rewarding. This is why I'm excited to continue my studies at the graduate level, to learn more and have an even bigger impact on the world.
