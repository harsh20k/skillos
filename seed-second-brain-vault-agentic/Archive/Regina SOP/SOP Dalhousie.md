### **Statement of Intent**

_**Master of Applied Computer Science**_


The amount of data being created is growing at a rate never seen before, along with Machine learning and other technologies to dig insights from this data, which has led to a transformation of almost all industries. I am fascinated by these technologies, like how my watch can accurately describe my sleep cycle or how a Boston Dynamics robot can do a flawless summersault. The inference of complex patterns from numbers and data sparked a curiosity about the underlying tech and algorithms through which these advancements work. This interest and my passion for technology and problem-solving helped shape my academic and professional paths, leading me to explore data, coding and creativity. 

In high school, I chose to study science, focusing specifically on computer science, which gave me the most joy at that young age. I also had an interest in physics and mathematics. Actively participating in computer competitions and discussions shaped my decision to pursue computer science as a core subject in my future education. My uncle, who was working at a renowned IT firm in the USA at the time, also inspired me with his success and dedication to the field. Soon, writing programs and learning logic systems became a very engaging process, and many of my nights were spent in front of C++ coding screens. Therefore, pursuing a Bachelor of Technology in computer science became a very natural decision for me.

---
This **four-year degree** enhanced my understanding of the foundational computing concepts and their various applications. During college vacations, I deep-dived into courses on advanced JAVA programming including web application development. In the fourth year of my undergraduate studies, I undertook a project in image processing and digital watermarking, exploring its applications in maintaining the integrity of digital ownership. I devised a solution using tools like MATLAB and Python that could help alleviate the challenges of copyright infringement. By experimenting with various algorithms, I started to realise that mathematics also plays a very crucial role in any significant technological endeavour. The project also broadened my perspective, and my interests began to include media and its applications. During my college studies, I also started learning video editing in my free time as a hobby and within no time, I started earning some pocket money from it. 

Beyond academics, I also participated in extracurricular activities that helped me better work in a team or as a leader and on tight deadlines. I served as an event coordinator and photography team lead, organising and capturing various college events through photography. Coordinating events and leading a photography team allowed me to channel my creative instincts.

---
After **graduation**, my professional journey began as a Systems Engineer at Infosys. I worked on large-scale software development and system optimisation projects there, collaborating with various teams. This gave me experience in real-world applications of JAVA and growing web technologies JS and APIs. I gained hands-on experience in programming, debugging and managing end-to-end software life cycles. My work on critical projects, such as optimising backend systems for an e-commerce client, reinforced my technical foundation and was also lauded; hence, I got promoted to senior system Engineer. I continued to refine my video editing hobby throughout this period and kept exploring new artistic avenues and software.

---
In 2015, I transitioned into freelancing in **animation and design**, where I have been working on diverse projects across various industries, such as music, advertising, and marketing. I got to work with so many great artists and innovators, and I learnt a lot by collaborating with them. A large portion of my portfolio involves hand-drawn animations for music videos and promotional campaigns. For example, in an advertising project for an eco-friendly brand, I created fully hand-drawn animations to illustrate the life cycle of sustainable products. In another project, I was involved in making dynamic visuals for a music video where I translated the lyrical narratives into animation using the word cloud generation through machine learning. These experiences strengthened my technical and creative skills, further inspiring me to pursue solutions that integrate computing with design.

To broaden my creative abilities, I learned to use tools like Adobe Suite, Blender, 3DS Max, and Maya, and also experimented with scripting to create interactive media. For example, I combined hand-drawn visuals with interactive elements using programming logic in a promotional video for a retail brand.  I actively took advanced programming courses and looked for creative ways to apply my technical skills to every project I worked on. This exposure inspired me to pursue business solutions that integrate computing with design. 

Additionally, I kept pursuing courses on the latest technologies to keep myself up to date. For example, I did a course in Data science using SAS, R and Python. Soon after, I wanted to create an application to manage my 300+ growing library or projects, so I took another course on [[Swift]] programming language and created apps to manage this data. Meanwhile, my sister, who started her master's program in data science in Canada, shared her great learning experiences and opportunities there. I also had some inclination to study further but was initially hesitant. As my understanding of technical and creative domains grew, my desire to formalise my knowledge through a master's program related to computer science also got reinforced.

---
 In the future, I see the growing importance of advanced fields in areas such as artificial intelligence, virtual reality and human-computer interactions. My work experience at Infosys equipped me with technical knowledge and a structured approach to problem-solving, while my freelancing career fostered creativity, adaptability and collaboration. Together, these experiences have motivated me to pursue further education, which would enable me to contribute to innovations that seamlessly blend technology with design.

After researching, I chose to pursue a Master of Applied Computer Science (MACSc) at Dalhousie University. Canada, known as a global hub for technological development, offers immense opportunities for learning and growth. Courses like Data Management and Advanced Cloud Architecting will deepen my understanding of cutting-edge solutions while Designing for User Experience and Mobile Computing will help me communicate complex data insights to diverse audiences in this mobile-first age. 

I am especially drawn to Dalhousie’s connection with [[DeepSense]], an innovative program that brings researchers and businesses together to solve problems in the ocean economy using Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. [[DeepSense]] will give me access to state-of-the-art resources like advanced cloud computing tools and guidance from industry leaders. Being part of such a program would give me a chance to work on real-world projects that have a meaningful economic impact, which fits perfectly with my goal of using technology to solve important challenges. Over time, I have come to recognise that my three core passions -learning, coding and design- are all integrated within this curriculum. Additionally, the Entrepreneurship option offers a chance to explore the business side of technology and turn ideas into real-world solutions.

---
After completing the program, I aim to return to India and work as a BI Analyst or Tech. Consultant. My long-term vision is to tackle challenges in industries like healthcare, education, and creative media by developing innovative solutions. Growing up, my mother taught me to value education and showed me how it can change lives. I feel lucky to have had the chance to attend good schools and colleges, but I know that many people don’t have the same opportunities. This has inspired me to work on solutions like personalised learning platforms to make education more accessible or diagnostic tools to improve healthcare for others. The knowledge and expertise I gain at Dalhousie University will add immensely to my career, empowering me to work through complex challenges and make meaningful contributions in these fields.

This program will help me gain a stronger understanding of cloud and mobile computing, and data management, giving me the skills and confidence to address practical issues in creative and effective ways. It will also sharpen my problem-solving skills and give me the tools to drive meaningful innovations.
