[[1. Please describe your specific area of academic interest, and explain what motivated you to choose this area.]]

#slipbox/permaNotes/SOP_Regina/1
I’m fascinated by data science and machine learning, and how they can turn huge amounts of raw information into insights that improve our everyday lives. It amazes me that clever algorithms can power wearable devices that track our sleep so accurately or guide robots to perform incredibly complex tasks. Finding patterns in data, predicting outcomes, and helping people make better decisions all feel exciting and meaningful to me.

My interest started during my undergraduate studies in computer science, where I learned the basics of programming, mathematics, and problem-solving. One of my most memorable projects was about image processing and digital watermarking, which showed me how theory can directly protect digital content in the real world. Seeing practical results from data-driven methods made me even more curious about what else could be done.

My career experiences have only strengthened this curiosity. At Infosys, I worked on optimising large software systems, which taught me how data can improve efficiency and reliability. Later, as a freelancer, I used machine learning to create animated visuals based on music lyrics, blending creativity and technology in a way I never thought possible.

What really motivates me is the potential to make a positive impact—helping businesses run more smoothly, improving medical diagnoses, or making education more accessible. Data science and machine learning bring together technical skills, logical thinking, and creative problem-solving, which I find both challenging and rewarding. That’s why I’m eager to pursue graduate-level studies, so I can gain deeper knowledge and make an even greater difference.
