# The Eternal Struggle and Divine Surrender

Its interesting that the strength we have to do good things is the same strength we use to do bad things, things that are not expected from us.

This dichotomy of good and bad is in our higher centres of the brain. But these primal instincts are deep rooted in our basal centres of our brain and very hard to master. With practice though we can win over them as described by Krishna in Geeta. No matter what we do we have to win over these primal urges to be the best version of ourselves. 

![[Pasted image 20250320101556.jpg]]

No one can help us be the best version of ourselves except God and Guru. But at the end of the day they help us remind that these bodily urges will not end and not lead to happiness. The responsibility however lies in our own hands whether to give in to these animalistic tendencies by identifying with the body or witness them as passing waves and be unattached to them without judgement and let them pass away. 

---
There is also one other way to try as best as we can and rest leave it to the [[Divine Mother]]. The divine mother will take care of the rest, she accepted us when we did not even try to fight these bad habits and routines, but now we are trying to get above these murky waters and we have to trust in the process she has laid out for us. 

No matter how many times we fall we will not give up. That's the attitude we all should carry within ourselves. When my mother wishes it it will happen in an instant and we can try all our lifetime and we will not be free.
