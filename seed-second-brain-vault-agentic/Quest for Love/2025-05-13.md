# The Path of Dharma And True Fulfilment

Its a different story all together. Spirituality and world wisdom are two very different things. A successful and well respected person in the world might not be spiritually advanced, similarly a spiritually advanced disciple might not necessarily mean worldly success and respect from everyone.

When we have to contest the world we can do so with our understanding but there is one more way, and that is the way of the *Dharma*. It outlines the actions and duties or selfless service one needs to do in any given situation. A person has a different dharma when interacting with their partner and a different dharma when in the presence of their children.

![[A serene image of a person meditating under a tree, with a subtle glow symbolizing spiritual enlightenment, surrounded by a world of chaos and responsibilities..jpg]]

Dharma encompasses every situation and it delineates the two right and wrong clearly for the devotee to follow. But inspite this dharma do not guarantee happiness or peace or anything like that, following dharma for its own sake is not enough on its own. The different dharma's of the world might not even give us a little bit of happiness, inspite of being a "good father/son/brother/husband". It might give the individual a recognition in the world, that they are so good parent and so on. But inside that doesn't mean that the individual might feel fulfilled. 

That fulfilment can only be had through God.
