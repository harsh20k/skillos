# The Divine Quest for Completion

The question of the aim of life is often not considered important by most of the individuals. Not even once, we stop and think why we are doing anything and where are we headed in the grand scheme of things. 

![](https://i.imgur.com/cttizY3.png)

 
We don’t have to stop everything in our life to think about the answer to these conundrums. The beauty of spirituality is that it most effortlessly answers these questions, in a way that does not disrupt our normal day to day lives. It is baffling then to see how many people don’t even want to think about these questions. 
 
Why man thinks he knows the answers to everything when he is suffering continuously, getting sickness or losses of wealth or loved ones? Even to get a job somewhere one has to learn skills working under someone, then it is strange why is it man thinks that he knows how to live? Scriptures answer each and every question and in many different ways conducive to every kind of temperament. 
 
My [[Guru]] has taught me that seeking God is our one and only objective, rest everything can wait. Only God can quench our primordial thirst hidden even from ourselves. That thirst is the proof that we are not complete, in spite of so much wealth, so many “loved ones” and friends, collected throughout our lifetimes. Then one should momentarily stop and think what is that I crave. What will make me feel complete. 
 
May [[God]] bless us with this knowledge and his love, that it might awaken in all of us the divine quest to seek Him.
