It is impossible to describe the true nature of Guru. Guru is indistinguishable from [[God]] himself.

Only God knows Guru and only Guru knows [[God]]. It might sound very paradoxical because our minds and hearts are by nature are comprised of natural elements and they are beyond the nature and yet exist in nature or [[Maya]]. 

![[Pasted image 20250111112835.jpg]]

Mind can be thought as a constitution of five elements so it can only grasp the ideas that are elemental. To grasp higher ideas [[God]]/[[Guru]] gives their power to us so we can grasp their level of comprehension.

We can still try to think, according to our capabilities the idea of this [[Truth]]. In the entire universe, the complete existence, there is only one source of [[Truth]]. [[Truth]] is God and as an extension Guru. So to understand the [[Truth]], the [[Truth]] incarnates itself in the form of our personal God and Guru. 

Everything other than the [[Truth]] is delusion or [[Maya]], which also has its origin based on the [[Truth]] itself. We as humans only are given the potential to try to understand the [[Truth]]. Or in other words no one except humans cannot even try to grasp the nature of [[Truth]]. Yet humans are also not capable of reaching the final step of realisation themselves, which is rooted in our elemental nature. 

When the [[Truth]] or God deems us worthy of knowing the [[Truth]], Guru or the [[Truth]] incarnate in flesh and body is given to the deluded individual. A divine relationship is formed when a Guru pledges to take the burden of disciple's life. This is relationship in [[Truth]] was already present but it is revealed for the benefit of the deluded [[soul]]. 

This marks as the end of the delusion of the individual. The [[Truth]] will slowly reveal itself as the individual will keep on surrendering to the divine Guru.
