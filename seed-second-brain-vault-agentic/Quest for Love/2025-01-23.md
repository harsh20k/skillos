# Tears of Devotion: The Path to Divine Union

Every thing that happens is the will of the [[God]], but everything that we do for our selfish desires is the will of the satan. 

My [[God]] never try to hide behind the veil of the world for the devotee who loves her. She is too kind to stay away from that devotee. She is too fragile-hearted to not feel for the devotee crying for her. 

I know that tear I shed for my [[Divine Mother]] is not wasted and she keeps those tears with great care near her feet. And in return she provides the devotee her highest knowledge of love and ultimate divine union of the devotee and his beloved.

![[Pasted image 20250123090950.jpg]]

---
The divine will is not merely known to the devotee who is devotee to [[Mother]] it is imbibed in his every action and thought. He feels the signature of [[Gurudeva]] in his every thought for the [[Divine Mother]] and feels the reassurance constantly that with the grace of his true gurudeva he has already found [[God]].

Everything we do throughout our day should be devoted to the [[Divine Mother]] be it studying, working, bathing, eating or any other mundane activity. Every action should be witnessed as a service to the [[Divine Mother]]. Sleeping should become an act of sleeping on the lap of the mother divine. Cleaning and bathing should become an act of cleaning the body and house for the [[Divine Mother]] so she will be happy to see his child living cleanly and hygienically. 

By doing this we break the rhythm of [[Maya]] and follow the divine rhythm of love and constant superconsciousness.

---
I was constantly confused with the idea of [[jeevan-mukta]]. I thought that it was a different state altogether, in which the individual becomes completely different than other people. 

---
But through [[Guru]]'s words I now know the actual state of jeevan-mukta. Speaking the name of the lord is being [[jeevan-mukta]]!!! Thinking of [[God]] and mother is that state of jeevan-mukta. If we are thinking about the world then we all are already dead. And if we are instead thinking of the [[Divine Mother]] and her will and how we can please her or anything about her we are jeevan-mukta at this very instant. 

---
Then I am a jeevan-mukta when I have "Rama - Rama" on my lips. I am a jeevan-mukta when I have tears in my eyes in the pain of being so far from divine mother.

Soon, the devotee will witness that by constant repetition of the lord's name he is jeevan-mukta always.
