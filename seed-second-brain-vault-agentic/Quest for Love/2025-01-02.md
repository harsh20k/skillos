# Navigating Maya: The Journey to Dharma

I like to think that I try to do my part, my responsibilities, my [[Dharma]]. Its not difficult to figure out what your real dharma is if you are connected to God. The true knowledge is given to the devotee through various means be it the immense curiosity of the devotee, or just a chance encounter with someone who gives the right knowledge at the right moment.

![[Pasted image 20250102094023.jpg]]

The real duty, the only duty of everyone is to find [[God]]. To look for the divinity everywhere, if we do not accomplish this task we will be punished after death, because that was our one and only duty during human life. Some might argue that there are other duties also, like taking care of the family, money etc. but they mistake the duty of the body with their duty. The body's dharma is to survive, to breathe, eat and drink, taking care of the well being of the family, however that is not "our" dharma. 

---
We are separate from the body but we forget this fact and keep doing the tasks only given to our body not to us. We tend to forget our true aim our objective and that causes us to suffer in innumerable ways. 

![[Pasted image 20250102094315.jpg]] 

When we have clear objective for the day then whatever day throws at us we have some idea how to navigate it, how to adapt and how to make the most of what we are given. Like that, if we have the objective in our sights at all times then throughout life whatever comes we will be able to smartly navigate, adapt and make the best of the circumstances. 

![[Pasted image 20250102094618.jpg]]

We make ourselves impotent when we lose our objective. We forget why we came here in the first place, and then start looking to satisfy ourselves with the worldly things that give us momentary pleasures. The pursuit of happiness is inbuilt in our minds and souls, its just that the direction we look for it is misguided by the selfishness of the body and senses. 

---
During our time here we all face the winds of [[Maya]]. We get mood swings, we get tossed by the three [[Gunas]] , [[sattv]], [[rajas]] and [[tamas]]. All these gunas affect the state of mind and body of the devotee, the emotions and well being included. But throughout these changes we must not forget their temporary nature. Their ephemeral nature, does not render them less powerful though. Once in a little cyclone of anger, one can do unimaginable things otherwise. We have to fix our gaze to the [[Divine Mother]] throughout these changes, and hope for her mercy to protect us. 
![[69263165-0358-4559-AC38-20B534A04B13_1_201_a.jpg]]
---
When the devotee has true devotion for [[God]] and [[Guru]] then he can face anything or any situation. All because he has his objective in his sights. He can clearly see why he came here so one failure or a momentary pleasure or pain might affect him for a very short timespan but in the long run he will succeed in his true goal or objective that is to find [[God]]
