# The Trust That Realises God

As described by [[Premandandji]] there are seven types of devotees. He explains all seven of them and categorised them broadly in three categories.

The first one is where the most of lie i.e. mild devotees. He described them as having a goal of meeting God but a lack of complete surrender to [[Guru]]'s teachings and an immovable trust that they will find God. 

---
Very interestingly he added that in spirituality when we think that we won't find [[God]] in this life then that will become true because we have trust in that. Just like that the opposite is also true that when a devotee has an unshakable trust in the [[Guru]] and [[Divine Mother]] that the goal he is aiming for that is god realisation, he will attain it in this life by the [[kripa]] of [[Mother]];the trust and surrender will make it true and in this life he will become God realised. 

![[Pasted image 20250201090622.jpg]]

What is the purpose of devotee then? I feel that most important thing in a devotee's life is to cultivate that trust and complete acceptance as a matter of fact kind of attitude towards the phenomenon of him meeting with the [[Divine Mother]]. With this complete trust he starts to pull on the divine with great force or we can say that [[Divine Mother]] when wants to meet with the devotee in this lifetime then she graces her child with that divine trust. 

---
### Nothing else remains then. Only living in that trust that sooner than we think we are about to meet our beloved mother. 

What we think we will achieve and what we think we will become. Then what's the point of delaying our own salvation. Just accepting the fact that we will soon be in our mother's lap will be our salvation.
