I asked my Professor of Advanced Topics in Software Development, Tushar Sharma, about my dilemna of making large number of tiny projects vs idea of less but substantial projects (whatever that means). 

He did not agree to either option but shared a very deep perspective. He mentioned the aim should be "Impact". The number of users of a project, the number of stars on the project's github and the number of people your solution is actually helping.

After some thought it sounds like a normal response, any learned person should say something along those lines, because what else is the aim of software, if not help people with something, right?! 

But yet it did not come naturally to me, my brain did not even bring it up as an option, because my mindset is completely opposite. I think like - if I somehow create a huge project then automatically I will have a big "audience". Writing it out feels cringe. But that's what felt natural to me🤮.

The contrast lies in the attitude an individual takes when building something. While one wants to make the biggest, baddest app for the iphone, the other one wants to help the most number of people in a specific problem. 

The success of both might look very similar on the outside, but it would be a complete 180 degree opposite philosophy up close.

While the approach let's say A seems to focus on inflating numbers, maximising margins or profits, the approach let's call it Z, simply relies on one thing "service".

But, the question still remains how does one shift their frame of reference from A -> Z? I DON'T KNOW! So I asked chatgpt 😅 and it gave some interesting points. 

1. **Awareness** – Notice your default mindset (“big = success”) vs. the alternative (“impact = success”). Naming the frame is the first shift.
    
2. **Reframe Metrics** – Track users helped, stars, and feedback instead of project size or complexity.
    
3. **Start Small but Real** – Build tiny, scrappy tools that solve real problems; feel the reward of helping.
    
4. **Shift Identity** – Move from “I am a builder proving skill” to “I am a problem-solver serving people.”
    
5. **Philosophical Anchor** – Treat software as _seva_ (service), not just a stage for self-expression.
    
6. **Gradual Path** – Expect to oscillate between big and small projects; each cycle nudges you toward impact-first instinct.

I won't claim to now have a deep mental shift or some epiphany, but yes its a good start to question and accepting that challenging viewpoint.
