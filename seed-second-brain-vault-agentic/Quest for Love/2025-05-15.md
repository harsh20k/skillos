# Meditation- A Gift, Not a Task

Along this journey in meditation practice the most beautiful memories are not of long 1 hour meditation where I could feel the bounds of my being being melted, but of the times when I could not sit and meditate and I just laid down on the floor and kept my head on the meditation mat thinking it as if divine mother's lap and talking to her, telling her about my inability to meditate and concentrate. Often at these times  I dozed off after a while. 

![[A serene meditation scene with a person lying on a mat, surrounded by divine light and nature, symbolizing peace and connection with the universe..jpg]]

When we shift the focus from the doer of meditation and become aware of the meditation as a gift from the universe, the divine mother, then we start reaping the real gifts. Gifts of contentment, peace, a solid foundation in character. 

Every suffering that we have been trying to escape were all just opportunities in disguise. The angry teammate, or the loud boss were presented to our consciousness to establish ourselves in peace in even such turmoil, when the body is in fight or flight response.

My Gurudeva says, "Everything else can wait, but our search for God cannot wait." That's what we have to do, in our hearts constantly remember God and do our duties with utmost care, results are never in our hands and never will be, with meditation you won't become a future teller. With concentration on God, we become attuned with the entire universe and every action of ours will become the act of devotion.
