# Yoga: The Universal Science of Knowing God

### The Purpose of Life

God sent us on earth to play in His drama of life for a while and not get entangled in it for incarnations, sizzling with untold misery. He wanted us to watch the drama of life with a clean mental attitude and then return to His eternal home of ever-new blessedness after briefly enjoying His stupendous show - not as a miserable mortal being, but as a blissful immortal, made in the image of God.

Daily scientific meditation is the most important aspect of your progress in realising your divine nature. It takes practice to become established in the basics of meditation before you can effectively apply the higher techniques that will be taught in later Lessons. Continue to review and apply each aspect of the methods taught in [[Lesson 1]]. The current Lesson will deepen your understanding of the path you have embarked upon and your motivation to pursue it with zeal. 

### What is Real Religion?

Religion, rightly understood, means that which fulfils the highest necessity of man - that which brings about the attainment of our essential goals and fulfilment of our _purpose_ on earth.[^1] 

If we analyse the actions of all human beings, ultimately we will find that everything we do - whether we are conscious of it or not - is motivated by a common, universal goal: the avoidance of suffering and the attainment of Bliss. Though there are many immediate needs and desires that stimulate our actions in daily life, behind all of them is the fundamental motivation to remove pain and find bliss. Every other human goal and desire is subservient to this one. 

Since this end is universal, me must consider it as man's highest necessity. And what is universal and most necessary for man is, of course, religion to him. Hence, religion _necessarily consists in the permanent removal of pain and the realisation of Bliss, or God._ And the actions that we must adopt to achieve this are the real, universal religion of man. 

In one sense everyone in the world is religious, since all are trying to get rid of want and pain and to gain Bliss. On the other hand, very few in the mad rush of daily life are _conscious_ of their ultimate goal, and even fewer know an effective mens for achieving it. 

Awake! Sleep no more! Don't continue to be one of the millions who stumble haplessly through life in a delusion-imposed stupor, unmindful of their purpose and potential! Often it is only when persons become afflicted with poverty or disease, and outer material methods of maintaining health, prosperity, and happiness fail, that they being to seek other means of release from their troubles. This is when they start to delve into various spiritual teachings. But for most persons, seeking a church or religion is usually a matter of curiosity, family custom, or going to whatever church is popular among their friends and associates. They may receive a little inspiration from songs or chants and sermons and beliefs, but all too often they become dogma-bound in the outer trappings of religion and cease to make a conscious effort to attain the Goal of Life.

### Eternal Truths That Supremely Benefit Mankind

The Sanskrit word [[Dharma]] (or _[[Dharma|Sanatana Dharma]]_, "Eternal Religion") signifies what religion or spirituality can be when freed from stagnation-inducing dogmatism, superstition, and the poisonous attitude that any one sect has an exclusive monopoly on the truth.

[[Dharma]], from the Sanskrit root *dhri*, "to uphold or support," is a comprehensive term fro the natural laws and eternal verities that uphold the divine order and harmony of the universe - and of man, a miniature universe. [[Dharma]] is defined as "those immutable principles which protect man permanently from the threefold suffering: physical disease, mental unhappiness, and spiritual ignorance."

From the vast body of eternal truth safeguarded in India and handed down from ancient times, three mystic philosophies have proven most vital in offering the methods of final emancipation of suffering humanity. They are called [[Sankhya]], [[Yoga]], and [[Vedanta]].[^2] Yogoda Satsanga teachings are a combination of these three, because together they constitute the true science of religion. 

The wisdom teachings of [[Sankhya]] give the _why_ of religion, and the practical methods of Yoga give the _how_. And [[Vedanta]], which means "knowledge of the end," teaches about the ultimate goal of life, the state of divine realisation to be reached through practice of Yoga.

[[Sankhya]] explains the reason one should learn and apply divine laws ([[Dharma]]). It points out that since our highest necessity in life is to eradicate all suffering of body, mind, and soul, we should adopt the most effective means of doing so. It says that ordinary human methods of diagnosing and treating human ills are inefficient, for they do not probe far enough into the real deep-seated cause of suffering. Therefore they cannot eradicate chronic physical, mental, and spiritual diseases and are powerless to permanently destroy man's susceptibility to future suffering.  ^c39675

This philosophy traces the cause of all occurrences of mental or spiritual suffering, as well as physical diseases and even accidents. It says that these do not happen by change, but because of the law of cause and effect ([[karma]]) as applied to our actions, governing not only this life but previous lives. It points out that God cannot be blamed for our ills, for He created us as divine beings and gave us free choice; and the reason that a child is born with some physical handicap, for example, is not due to the arbitrary edict of a Divine Tyrant, but because of some self-created karmic tendency dragged by that soul from a past life.

Metaphysically, no human being can be considered free from suffering as long as there exists in him the latent seeds of suffering. Elimination of suffering, according to [[Sankhya]], must mean removal not only of present ills, but of any possibility of future misfortune. Without that assurance man is plagued by the fear and uncertainty that no matter how good things seem today, there is no guarantee about tomorrow! Therefore, real religion should enable each individual to destroy the _causes_ of threefold sufferings - to remove them by the roots, so that they can never sprout or grow again. 

Such a definition of religion makes it a panacea for the followers of all so-called different religions. It tells us that no matter whether we follow any of the world's religions or none, we all should know those principles of living which would permanently destroy our sufferings. These principles are not a matter of dogmatic belief, but of scientifically understanding our true nature and innate divine potentials.

So [[Sankhya]] urges you to make the goal of life conscious. And [[Vedanta]] describes the goal to be attained: the joyous union with the eternally satisfying Bliss-God that is experienced after the sickness of delusion is permanently healed. But [[Yoga]], the most important of the three, acquaints you with the actual methods that bring about that healing - explaining how, by contacting the Infinite Bliss of Spirit, you can forever banish the threefold sorrows from the shore of your soul.

### Keep the Goal Always in Mind

When you apply yourself to achieving a material goal such as acquiring money, your efforts are stimulated by your knowledge of _why_ you are seeking prosperity: You know that money will provide food, shelter, and the other necessities of life - as well as fulfilling cherished material desires - and therefore you pursue those ambitions with zeal. But rare is the individual who tries with comparable motivation and single-mindedness to solve the enigmas of life and attain its supreme goal.

To assure speedy progress in your spiritual quest, dear student, you must continuously concentrate on _why_ you are following the path of Yogoda Satsanga. Be thoroughly convinced of the absolute necessity of undertaking a scientific spiritual search, by remembering always its purpose and end. 

First, convince your reason that the ever0existing, ever-conscious, ever-refreshing Bliss-God is the heart's ideal of every human being. Then, constantly hold this thought before your mind's eye during every action, during every act of meditation, until you can remain always merged in the everlasting, ever-entertaining bliss of God within you. The superconscious ecstasy of contact with God is the only thing that can truly quench the desire-thirst and happiness-hunger of the soul.

## Yoga Is the Spiritual Science of Liberation

### Rising Above Identification With the Body

Until you know yourself as Spirit - the fountainhead of Bliss - you are limited by the mortal body and inexorably subject to the constantly changing dualities of mortal existence: pleasure and pain, happiness and sorry, health and sickness, success and failure, good and evil, life and death. Knowledge of your true being brings eternal freedom. 

Yoga teaches that the divine soul, through contact with the changeable bodily environment during the state of wakefulness, ascribes the limitation of the body unto itself. Because of this, the changes of the body and of its outer environment keep mans' consciousness in a constant state of excitation or restlessness. 

Your restless mind is a veil of ignorance between you and God. When you are calm, your consciousness becomes transparent, revealing God. When you are calm, you feel His image within.

[[Yoga]] consists of the actual methods for rising above our narrow identification with the mortal body and restless mind, and identifying ourselves with the calm, immortal soul. In this way alone can we awaken intuition - the superconscious knowing-power of the soul - which is the only faculty capable of directly perceiving Reality: Spirit or God.

### The Universality of Yoga

Various religious practices for achieving body - and ego-transcendence are found in all the great religious traditions of the world - known to the illumined prophets, saints, and mystics. However, in all religions there is an outer shell and an inner core. The exoteric worship - is what is taught to the masses; the esoteric core - consisting of disciplines that bestow actual experience of God - was often veiled in symbolism and not understood or practiced by many.

Few religious beliefs exist that have not already been tested in India; indeed, most of the effective esoteric teaching have been inspired directly or indirectly by Indian philosophy. 

Ancient documents indicate that fifteen of the eighteen unaccounted- for years in the early life of Jesus were spent in India and Tibet with Hindu _rishis_ and Buddhist monks. His entire teaching is in close accord with [[Vedanta]] and [[Yoga]] truths.[^3] However, it does not matter from what place or from whom truth emanates. Truth itself if our primary concern.

Since ancient times, India's spiritual scientists have investigated every known method for transcending the limited ego and body consciousness - the root cause of man's physical, mental, and spiritual suffering as well as of his apparent separation from the bliss of God. These methods were systematised into the various branches of [[Yoga]] (outlined in the section "Different Branches of Yoga" in the "Handbook and Glossary for Students of the YSS Lessons"). The discoveries of the yogis have been practiced and proven through centuries of research and experimentation. In [[Yoga]], therefore, we find a truly universal science of knowing God.

Even in India, however, much of the highest esoteric teaching of Yoga was lost during the Dark Ages; and the profound religion of the [[Vedas]] became, for the masses, a matter of external ritual and superficial belief.[^4] In the Yogoda Satsanga teachings and techniques, you are taught the distilled essence - freed of obscuring symbolism - of the most effective methods from all the branches of Yoga.

### Scientific Yoga Works With the Life Force

In a broad sense, yoga can be considered any systematic discipline whose result is the transcending of the body and ego.[^5] In the highest sense, though, yoga means the Eightfold Path of [[Raja Yoga]], with its techniques of [[Pranayama]] (life-force control). The most efficacious form of [[Pranayama]] is the [[Kriya Yoga]] technique given to the world in the modern era by the Gurus of [[Yogoda Satsanga Society of India|YSS of India]].

The superiority of [[Pranayama]] over other methods lies in the fact that it works with the exact thing that binds us down to our narrow individuality - the _life force_. 

All religious methods in the world directly or indirectly, tacitly or expressly, enjoin the control, regulation, and turning back of the life force so that we may transcend the body and mind and know the Self in its native state. Savants of different ages and climes have suggested methods adapted to the mental frame and condition of the people among whom they lived and preached. Some have laid stress on prayer, some on feeling, some on good works, some on love, some on reason or thought, some on meditation. But their motives have been the same. They all meant that body consciousness should be transcended by the turning back of the life force inward, and the Self should be realised, as the image of the sun appears in calm, unruffled water.

### The Quickest Path to God

One can travel from Los Angeles to New York by many different means - by walking or going by bullock cart, ship, auto, train, or airplane. By bullock cart, it would take several months; but by airplane you can arrive in a matter of hours. Likewise, various spiritual teachings, according to their semi-developed or highly developed training, can lead you to God slowly or quickly. I call the Yogoda Satsanga/Self-Realisation teachings the "airplane way" to God.

By mechanical, ardour-less, soulless, social attending of church or temple, it would take multiple incarnations to reach God. The great yogi [[Patanajali]] says: "The most ardent find quickest realisation."[^6] Jesus similarly commended those who love God with all their heart (feeling and devotion), all their mind (concentration), all their soul (possible only after finding the soul through meditation-awakened intuition), and all their strength (withdrawing "strength" or life force from the sense and directing it to the centres of divine perception in the spine and brain, in order to enter mystic ecstasy or [[Samadhi]]-union with God.)

The techniques of yoga (union of soul and God), taught in these [[Yogoda Satsanga Lessons]], scientifically enable any seeker to become that kind of devotee.

By yoga I do not mean simply _Hatha, Jnana, Bhakti, Karma, Mantra,_ or any of the other separate branches of Yoga; but rather the universal system of [[Raja Yoga]] - the sum of all Yogas. It was to this that [[Bhagavan Krishna]] referred when he proclaimed Yoga as the highest path:

"_The Yogi is deemed greater than body-disciplining ascetics, greater even than the followers of the path of wisdom or of the path of action; be thou, O Arjuna, a yogi!"[^7]

## Patanjali's Eightfold Path of Raja Yoga

India's great sage [[Patanjali]] outlined the [[Raja Yoga]] system of Hindu philosophy as an Eightfold path.[^8] The Yogoda Satsanga teachings provide you with the methods and guidance for ascending the eight steps of this Path.

The first steps, (1) *yama* and (2) *niyama*, require observance of five proscriptive moralities and five prescriptive rules. 

The next steps are (3) *asana* (right posture): the spinal column must be held straight and the body firm, in a comfortable position for meditation; (4) _pranayama_ (control of _prana_ subtle life currents); and (5) *pratyahara* (interiorization of the mind resulting from conscious withdrawal of the life force from the senses). 

The final steps are forms of yoga itself: (6) *dharana* (concentration, or holding the mind to one thought); (7) *dhyana* (meditation); and (8) *samadhi* (superconscious perception).

### First step: Yama (Proscriptive Rules)

_Yama_ means that which is prohibited, specifying thoughts and actions from which the devotee should abstain: injury to others, untruthfulness, stealing, incontinence (lack of control of the sexual impulse), and covetousness. 

### Second Step: Niyama (Prescriptive Rules)

_Niyama_ means that which the devotee should do. The _niyama_ prescripts are: purity of body and mind, contentment, self-discipline, self-study (contemplation), and devotion to God and Guru. 

All religions agree to the need for such a code of moral and disciplined behaviour as a foundation for religious practice. To meditate without simultaneously practicing _yama-niyama_ is like building a giant structure on a weak foundation. 

_Yama-niyama_, the "do's and don'ts", are the basic principles of every religion. However, one cannot find God by morality alone. 

Many religionists are content merely to practise these proscriptive and prescriptive rules: "I have never done anything wrong; I have done only good things in my life." one who lives by this standard gains some inward satisfaction and some good karma (results or effects of actions), but progresses little beyond that. You can be moral without being religious, but you cannot be religious without being moral. 

The earnest seeker who would reach God must follow all steps outlines by Patanjali in the Eightfold Path. Practice of the principles of _yama-niyama_ provides the firm moral and spiritual support essential for mastery of higher steps. Only when _nirvikalpa samadhi_ has been attained - that is, when one's consciousness is firmly and unshakeably established in God - does one go beyond _yama-niyama_, not before. [[Bhagavan Krishna]] instructs the devotee: "One who is united to cosmic wisdom goes beyond the effects of both virtue and vice, even here in this life. Therefore, devote thyself to yoga, divine union. Yoga is the art of proper action."[^9]

### Third Step: Asana (Practice of Motionlessness)

[[Patanjali]] states that in addition to _yama-niyama_ the devotee must practise _asana_, or posture - _correct_ posture. Stillness is the altar of Spirit, and Patanjali says that right posture leads to stillness, in which there is perfect ease of body and mind. Where motion ceases, Spirit begins. When the body and mind become totally still, one begins to perceive the manifestation of Spirit. Right posture enables the devotee to rise above or halt the motions of the body that cause restlessness in the ego-identified soul. 

The soul is a part of the calm Infinite, and the body a part of restless Nature. But the soul identifies itself with the restless body and forgets that its essential nature is Spirit. Therefore, to transport the prodigal soul from distractive restlessness to its home in infinite calm, correct posture is imperative - a posture in which the body is still and free from restlessness, and the spine erect. An erect spine allows life energy and consciousness to flow freely from the sense upward through the spinal centres to the higher centres of spiritual realisation in the brain. 

Patanjali points repeatedly to the purpose of spiritual actions and tells the devotee not to practise any method of salvation as an end in itself, but to use devotedly the method they have chosen until salvation is attained. Patanjali warns devotees not to be so engrossed in the process that they lose sight of the purpose for which that process or technique was prescribed. Many devotees, for example, are content to progress no further than the limited accomplishment of _asana_. Such persons devote themselves to the physical discipline of _Hatha Yoga_, enjoying the suppleness, vitality, and mastery of the body that _asana_ afford them, forgetting that the real purpose of _asana_ is to enable the devotee to attain freedom of the mind and soul. Therefore Patanajali does not prescribe lengthy concentration on the practice of various postures.

The correct posture or _asana_ for meditation is any comfortable position in which the spine is erect, the body steady and relaxed, and the mind undisturbed.

### Fourth Step: Pranayama (Life-Force Control)

Once a steady posture and a steady mind are attained, the devotee is advised by Patanjali to practise _pranayama_, for the purpose of attaining _pratyahara_ (interiorization of the mind). 

_Pranayama_ means conscious control of the life force (the body's creative, life-sustaining, and life-activating energy). To raise the consciousness to higher states of divine perception, the devotee must be able to halt at will the flow of life force into the five senses and, ultimately the heart and other vital organs, all of whose functions keep the soul identified with the body. The energy is then redirected to the centres of life in the spine and brain, through whose activities one is able to lift up the consciousness and commune with God.

Various schools utilizing meditation, breath control, chanting, devotional singing, the prayer wheel, "holy rolling," concentration by discrimination or by diversion, and other devices have emerged as offshoots of the real science of _pranayama_. The varied methods advocated by these schools are indirect ways of transferring the mind from the senses to God. The _pranayama_ technique of yoga teaches the scientific, direct way to disconnect the mind from sensations of smell, taste, sight, touch, and sound, by switching off the life current from its physiological occupations, freeing the consciousness to seek and unite with God.

All scientific techniques that bring about union of soul and Spirit may be classified as yoga: _pranayama_ is the most effective yoga method for attaining this divine union. 

***Sleep, or Unconscious Pranayama***. The state of sleep is unconscious [[Pranayama]], or the unconscious process of switching off the life force from the five senses. In sleep one practises the "corpse pose," savasana. In this posture the body is still; the muscles have stopped working; there is a decrease in the waste products in the venous blood, so heart action slows, and the energy in the heart becomes quiet. The life force thereby is switched off from the five senses, and the mind attains _pratyahara_ (withdrawal) unconsciously; that is, it automatically becomes focused within and absorbed in a subconscious state of peace. 

Every night in sleep the Lord shows you that your real Self cannot be bound by consciousness of this mortal form. Every night, He dissolves your body-consciousness and makes you realise that you are not a man nor a woman, but pure consciousness, an individualised reflection of His consciousness, sleeping in space, in joy. Every night, subconsciously, you become unlimited. In the day you exist as a human being, and in the nighttime you exist as the image of God. Yoga says: By meditation, establish that freedom and body-aboveness in waking consciousness, too; because body-identification is the source of all suffering.

The scientific method by which the mind can be directly and consciously disconnected from sensations is an important step on the Eightfold Path of Patanjali. The switching-off of the life current, unconsciously attained during sleep, can be attained consciously by adopting scientific methods of [[Pranayama]]. The science behind _pranayama_ will be explained in the Lesson on the Hong-Sau Technique ([[Lesson 4]]). The Energization Technique (taught in [[Lesson 6]]) also is a form of _[[Pranayama]]_, for practice of these exercises employs the principle of conscious control and direction of the life force. Mastery of the Energization Exercises is very helpful to the practice of Yogoda Satsanga meditation techniques, including [[Kriya Yoga]].
