# Foundations of Truth and Harmony: A Reflection on Marriage

Now the dates of my wedding are being discussed among the elders.

It is a good time to reflect on the meaning of marriage and the institution of a family. First of all it is important to reflect on why thinking about marriage is important in the first place. 

Just like undertaking any thing in life we should weigh its pros and cons and then take an informed decision and try our best to stick with our decision no matter it might become along the way, its a good thought to reflect on this important institution which is the cornerstone of the current cultural society of the present day.

Marriage in my opinion is a vow till death two people make to walk side by side hand in hand. As humans we all are social but how do we organise such hyper-social life with our duties and responsibilities. Marriage in essence i believe solves this problem by assigning a partner with which we can share everything(possibly the truth), and then living with them and creating the social boundary of a family create an invisible fence around us in which we can then thrive to fulfil our duties and responsibilities.

![[Pasted image 20250114095008.jpg]]

We are of course individuals first so our top most responsibility is to take care of the well being of this individual as a parent cares for their children. When we create this fence around us through marriage it is for our own benefit so that in a world full of millions of individuals we get to know one and only one to their core, that gives us the freedom to explore our instinct of affection and care but also gives us enough room to fulfil our highest responsibilities. 

---
In marriage the foundation should always be truth, just like for any meaningful relationship for example the relationship of me with myself. If that foundation is corrupted with lies and facades the marriage is nothing but a door to hell. The lies after corrupting this relationship will take hold of both the individuals that are facilitating this kind of corruption and engulf their hearts and corrupt the very core of their being which will be very hard to clear or even look at.

---
If we take a vow of truth then we not only save this relationship from corruption but we also help our own individual self from being condemned into perpetual rot. No amount of affection and "love" can survive once this corruption takes root. So no matter what the vow of truth is the most important of them all. All other vows inherit from this main vow. 

---
As [[Premanandji]] says through a spiritual marriage it is very easy to find [[God]] which is our highest goal and objective. A harmonious marriage rooted in the truth will help both of the persons and nudge them towards the final truth of divinity within.
