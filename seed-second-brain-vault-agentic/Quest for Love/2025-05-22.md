# Whatever We Seek: Higher Power Provides

_True wealth comes not from attachment, but from surrender to the higher power that always provides._

All of us seek something or the other, in fact we seek many things of the world. Our approach however has been flawed.

We carry these responsibilities about money, family, home, car and superficial things on top of our shoulders. A spiritual man, however who has the same responsibilities give up all of these to a higher power.

Spiritualising our thinking can take away first the weight of these "big things". Then comes a time when we in every situation are centred on divinity and we perform our duties perfectly.

Through centred consciousness on the Divine Universe the daily life might not change much, but in our heart we will start feeling that bliss, that joy which is the root cause of our being. 

![[A serene image of a person meditating under a tree, surrounded by nature, with a glowing aura symbolizing connection to a higher power.jpg]]

Calling from Higher Power makes the individual less selfish and all his actions are done by weighing them against the scales of right and wrong. 

When the actions are in tune with the universal laws his past karma start to break apart slowly and his devotion to the universe increases even further.

Let's say a person who has been driving a bike now has a family and now needs a car, his first thought will be to earn enough to afford it. 

The same thing will happen in a spiritual man's life but the difference is his complete consciousness is absorbed in the divine while doing it. 

**Spiritual = unattached to the outcome, 
Non-spiritual = completely attached to the outcome.**

The difference is simple the devotee will start to save money for the car for his family. He does this with intelligent use of his time and energy.

The other person however, will start doing what he feels deep down is not right, maybe he will start to charge more and unfairly, and cutting corners in his job or try some get rich quick schemes. 

The attachment to the outcome, makes a person blind to the universal laws of right and wrong. Anyone who is suffering from attachment can never be kind, can never truly love someone, nor can he ever serve his family or friends with compassion.

### Mother and Her Two Sons

A simple story illustrates this beautifully. A mother buys two oranges thinking of her two sons. She gets home and gave one to each one of her loved little ones. One of them starts comparing the orange size and colour with the second orange, when satisfied goes to the table and start eating the delicious orange.

The second son however, takes the orange, peels it and with gratitude thinks that the mother who gave me this should eat it first. He goes to his mother and puts one piece in her mouth. The mother's heart melted and she puts the son on her lap and feeds him with her own hands.

---

Is that mother partial? Doesn't she love both her sons equally?

She loves both equally but, the difference in the mindset of the two make it look that she loves one more than the other. 

The universe gives us so much, sunlight, fresh air, water, food and the energy in the organs to digest and assimilate this food. 

What an intelligent approach would be - is the acknowledgement of this Higher Power and draw from this *source of abundance and love* and the universe will love us back even more. 

Or we can take our orange and sit on the table and eat it proudly. The universe doesn't care, like the mother, it will keep giving but we cut off that flow of love by not being grateful.

Yes, we have many problems in life, but that doesn't mean we cannot find a single moment for the "Source of Everything". 

We by ourselves can never stop the cycle of suffering with its constant ups and downs, but with the help of the universe everything is possible. 

**Then it is our highest duty to sit for sometime and talk to that "Higher Power"- that divine source of our being and this multifaceted world.** 

Meditation is a fundamental change in one's life. Its like a u-turn on the road of life. We stop chasing the things of the world and instead loving the source of everything we have received.

We compare our houses with the neighbours', our bank accounts with others'. What good does it do us, only more misery and suffering. 

Through changing our consciousness from worldly to spiritual, we can enjoy what we have and the universe will take care of what we need and put that thing on our lap.
