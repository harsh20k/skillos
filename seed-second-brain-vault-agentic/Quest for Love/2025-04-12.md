# The Power of Surrender: Where Mercy Awakens

All this sadhana or spiritual practices lead to the same conclusion, that without the mercy of the divine mother nothing can be achieved. 

One might be a celibate and enjoy the various fruits of that practice but until the divine mercy bestowed upon the child nothing is actually accomplished.

The powerlessness felt by the devotee is like the concentrated [[Kripa]] flowing on the devotee. This translates into complete surrender of everything that the devotee thought was his and giving up of all the strength at the feet of the [[Mother]]. 

![[Pasted image 20250412104055.jpg]]

This surrender purifies the deepest corners of the soul which have not seen the divine light for a very long time. At the end of this all that remains is the utmost mercy of the divinity. Just like the example of Draupadi, when the soul has many different strengths and thinks it does not need the divine intervention, until then mother stays hidden behind this veil of ignorance. At the very instant when the complete hopelessness takes over the devotee at that very instant divine mercy or my mother's love can't stay hidden anymore. 

All the knowledge of the world, all the knowledge of the vedas and all the powers of the divine are at the disposable of such a devotee, who has given up everything.
