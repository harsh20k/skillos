# "Only Love"

This thought is coming to me again and again. Why did [[Paramhansa Yogananda]] say **"Only love can take my place"**? With immense importance and stress he puts in the techniques and teachings, he could have said something similar for "teachings" or "techniques"; but instead he uses the term "Only", only before "Love". 

Its interesting to note that a book by **[[Daya Mata]]** is also is titled **"Only Love"**, like she has taken direct inspiration from this declaration of Guruji.

Until now I have not read or heard any statement like this in the Guruji's works, with an unequivocal impetus on a single subject. I haven't heard anything similar said in relation to other subjects like __"Only meditation", "Only Techniques", "Only Kriya Yoga", "Only Karma Yoga", "Only Dharma" or "Only Teachings/ Lessons"__. 

---

A similar declaration is made in [[Gita]] by [[Sri Krishna]] that ***"Leaving all the dhramas, surrender your entire being to only me".***

[[Premanandji]] has made the same declaration thousands of times saying that **if we can't perform bhajan it will be fine if we have that inner surrender to our Guru and Ishta**. 

All these statements are pretty unequivocal and one sided and quite clear on the object of their stress. This is not merely a coincidence. It is the masters and Gurus repeating the same divine law, that surrendering, loving and having devotion for [[Divine Mother]] is the only way out of this Samsara. 

![[Pasted image 20250412104259.jpg]]

What I believe is that all the techniques and teachings of Gurudeva are the shortcuts or quick ways to arrive at this restful place of "Love". 

I think it is a very bold claim and also comprised of a charming restfulness. There are so many teachings of Guruji, numerous techniques and kriyas and mudras. The introductory qualifications of Yoga are also comprised of numerous do's and don'ts. 

---

How can a hapless devotee like the most of us can even begin to follow all this? 

As described by [[Premanandji]] all the spiritual practices are the ways to completely destroy the ego of the devotee. **When after a long faithful practice of any path of yoga every devotee must come to the same conclusion that without the grace of God and Guru, all the practices will only puff his ego consciousness which will keep him bound to the body consciousness.** Following this conclusion the only way that is left with the devotee is "complete giving up/ complete surrender/ the final crying". The devotee giving up all the ego of his [[Sadhana]] cries for the divine mercy, cries to the universe about his powerlessness, cries to the [[Divine Mother]] that he cannot find a way to her, it has to be her who can get to her child.
