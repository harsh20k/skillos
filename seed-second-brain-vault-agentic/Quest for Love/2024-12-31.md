# The Path of Devotion: A Journey Towards Divine Connection

The reason of continuous falling from [[Dharma]] into [[Adharma]] is not doing [[naam-jap]]. Only constant remembrance of the divine can save us from such wrong-doings. If we could do that all alone then why haven't we done that yet? We cannot that's why we need this ultimate tool to leave all the pleasures of the world and body and stand uprights as a true devotee. 

By doing constant naam-jap we will get the courage to fight the bodily urges and we will leave such things like an elephants garland falls off. That's how these saints can leave their body with full consciousness and without any suffering, because they do not identify themselves as the body, but something much subtler.

![[Pasted image 20241231084426.jpg]]

---

This can be done by following the [[Guru]]'s teachings faithfully and trying to do constant [[naam-jap]]. Then why haven't I incorporated this in my schedule?

Actually I have added it as an hourly/half-hourly reminder on my phone and that is helping me to at least make a simple habit of doing it. Then slowly I will aim for longer periods of naam-jap and then for constant naam-jap. 

During the time of [[Satsanga]] I should not distract myself into doing other pointless things like playing chess or admin tasks. My only focus should be on the satsanga and maybe do with it naam-jap or haung-sau technique as described by my [[Gurudeva]]. 

These small changes will help me better prepare myself for the meeting with my divine mother if she wishes. Forget about her, I will keep saying the name she loves the most and wait for her. I lover her, that is my only super-power. She can do anything she is the owner of the universe. I have to be a good boy and try to get her attention through the sadhana prescribed by the [[Guru]].
