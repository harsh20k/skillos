# Walking the Divine Path: A Journey to True Happiness

In my life I was inspired by other great people and their life stories. The decisions and choices they made in difficult situations were particularly interesting to me. I wanted to be a great man always and the most happy person I can ever become. 

This is the search most people start with but hardly comprehend or think about. They act instinctively and without a second thought putting no critical judgement on themselves and their direction. 

---
Through this search I stumbled on various gems I thought were the most valuable at the moment and I gave myself to them. I thought programming was one of them, but soon I realised the cap of my own understanding did not allow me to excel in that. Then I thought balancing with the various hedonistic pleasures will provide some happiness but that turned out to be really hollow. After that I found a very interesting idea of looking for one's own passion. I tried that but that also was a dead end. 

![[Pasted image 20250120085235.jpg]]

Throughout this process of finding my way it turned out was the whole plan of [[Divine Mother]] who was directing my life with immense detail and care. This whole plan was to bring me her son closer to her and lo! I started walking towards her. 

---
That is the divine mercy it is always trying to bring everyone closer to the unity the ultimate truth and divinity. 

No matter what I was very stubborn to find my passion and achieve great things by following it and I did some remarkable work and earned good money doing it. But was I happy? The answer was very clear to me. Not even once I felt rested and peaceful and felt that this is happiness. 

---
Only when I started on the true path, the path followed by all the yogis and rishis and the blessed ones did I start feeling some real solace, some real peace and true love. Oh! this is real happiness every time I feel the presence of [[Divine Mother]] I feel the same way. The thought of living without her feels disgusting and animalistic and worse than death itself.
