# The River of Words: Writing as a Sacred Habit

Writing can be sometimes hard when I start to think when to write or what to write. When it is done just a normal habit like brushing teeth then it is done exactly like that like a habit.

Its okay to not know what I am going to write about before writing in advance. As I start thinking and words start flowing it makes its own current or thought river where I just have to witness what I am going to write. Then worrying about the topic of a writing session is pointless.

![[Pasted image 20250220112207.jpg]]

Ultimately I have one topic on which I can write about at length at any point of time. My divine mother is inexhaustible and I cannot stop ever writing about her or my own experiences with her. All the rivers of my thoughts and words originate from the same source and end at the same ocean the reservoir or knowledge that is my mother's divine feet.

---
Since I always have something to fall back to write on the art of writing becomes merely an exercise in maintaining a powerful habit for long period of time without fail. When I do that I start to distance myself completely from this process of thinking about writing, thinking about what to write about and the actual writing. From a distance this all process seems very interesting but I am aloof to the whole drama of writing. 

Writing purifies the mind and relieves it from the knots formed in our life due to wrong living and thinking processes which have taken hold in forms of stubborn habits. 

No matter what we do externally these knots these ignorance laden thought bundles formed by repeatedly doing the same wrong thing, can only be destroyed by repeatedly doing the same right thing, by repeatedly thinking about the same right thing and by extension writing about the same right thing. This process of acting out the same right thing will keep us grounded in our reality and make us a better person day by day without putting a lot of effort which is too costly otherwise.

---
So the daily habit of writing is like buying a very expensive item and paying not in a big lump-sum but in very tiny instalments every day, because paying it at once is just not humanly possible. Just like there is limit on our UPI accounts for daily transactions, we have limits on our daily mental expenses.
