# Divine Alignment: A Devotee’s Pursuit of Love and Remembrance

How often do I forget [[Love]]. How often do I forget to love. The crazy thing about this is no matter I do can't be sure but mostly I still feel that I am missing something unless I have [[God]] in my consciousness. 

![[Pasted image 20241216091015.jpg]]

This experience is the proof that I am one of the favourites of my [[Divine Mother]]. [[Premanandji]] says that we should ask the divine mother for her 
**[[nirantar bhajan]]** always. And he adds that requests like these are often answered by god positively very quickly. 

Now I should request from the divine consciousness only two things divine love and nirantar bhajan. I have been blessed with a little taste of the divine love but I can't imagine when my whole being will be blessed with its touch!! The day will soon come and I have goosebumps just thinking about it. 

Its strange that we get happy or sad because of outward circumstances, but it doesn't bother us how much [[Sadhana]] we might have done that day!

We listen to [[Satsanga]] and understand the importance of sadhana and how our goal is to actually love God with all our hearts and all our souls, yet the ignorance of assuming happiness in the world still remains deep rooted in our conscious or subconscious minds. This [[Ignorance]] is because we still base our existence on our physical bodies. Its a very far fetched thought for us that we have bodies but we are beyond the physical body and the subtle mind. 

Satsanga is like taking a pill to remove this disease of erroneous thought process. No matter how much we try if we don't get blessing from the infinite to receive satsanga we have no chance in overcoming our limited body problem. 

**Things to do everyday are simple.**
- Love God
- Remember God
- Ask for his Love
- Ask for his Remembrance

How simple could be life then, if we remember what's important all the time. Facing everything that comes at us with a smile the devotee is rooted in his actual pursuit of happiness and does performs his duties and responsibilities with dispassion and like machine. 

When we have only his consciousness then we stop asking from the world anything. We have everything at the moment when our consciousness is engaged with the divine.
