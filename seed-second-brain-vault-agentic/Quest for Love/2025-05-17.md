# The Art of NOT BEING RIGHT

Everyone has a different story shaped by their unique experiences. Isn't it immature then to expect everyone to come to a consensus at any single event or piece of new knowledge. 

Of course, one event is objectively just one event and nothing more. The number of observers are many however, and that leads to that same incident being interpreted in so many ways. Our opinions cannot be divorced from our life experiences that we have had up until that moment. 

Establishing peace in such case might feel elusive or even impossible. I see an object and I see it this way and this way only. You will see it in different light and so does the person next to you. Inspite these differences there is still unity in the underlying process of observation and interpretation.

What's common between everyone is that they interpreted a thing in a way that was according to their place, time and state of mind. With this understanding there can be a reasonable dialogue between the opposing viewpoints without burning all the communication bridges. 

![[Pasted image 20250517103900.jpg]]

This insight is one of the foundations of spiritual life. When we give up anger or greed, what we are doing is intentionally giving up the needs of our mind to prove them right. We become open to almost anything. So what that this person completely disagrees with me, so what this person only thinks about themselves. 

The open-minded will be more humble not because he is better, but because he knows the common traps in all this drama. He has witnessed the same unwavering selfishness in himself, that need to prove himself right at all costs. When you have accepted your own dark side, humility follows naturally. Just like knowledge makes the ignorant polite, knowledge of the universe make the person a servant of the universe. To serve all is his sole purpose. 

Its like comparing notes after looking at an art piece. There are no wrong answers. But if its the first time you are looking at art, the powerful emotion you felt must be true for everybody! Right? Of course that is not the case, when you have looked at enough art and asked enough people about their opinions the only conclusion you can come to is "art is subjective." Life is like that everyday we encounter differences among ourselves and others, but what maturity brings is humility from the understanding that I am not right neither the other person; we all are merely seeing what we want to see, we are projecting our own emotions to every single things that happens.

Let's try to cultivate that understanding in our everyday lives which can only happen with the remembrance of God and [[Divine Mother]]. The underlying love and mercy in the universe does not discriminate among the ignorant and the learned. It does not discriminate via colour of the skin or the quality of thoughts. We all are her's forever and ever. 

With this understanding its impossible to get angry even when completely opposing view is presented. Like a mother who knows how different each and every child is different does not discriminate because they all belong to her, they all are her own beloved children.
