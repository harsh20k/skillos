# Surrender to the Divine: A Sharnagati Experience

I am witnessing a turning point in my [[Sadhana]]. When there is some sort of total dependence([[Sharnagati]]) of the devotee towards the divine, the small problems, like a simple task that might come up whenever you are about to meditate does not feel like a hindrance at all! 

On the face value, it might not sound like much, but from experience I can say that it has made a big impact in my daily life, thanks to the [[Divine Mother]] and her immense [[kripa]]. 

> [!NOTE]
> I can't really point to a point when it started to happen but it is just now that I am able to see it clearly. 
> 

![[Pasted image 20241227094552.jpg]]

---
It is just a minor shift in framing the problem at hand. For example, let's say we are trying to do something really important, like meditation or studying for something urgent and suddenly a call comes in for a yet another important task, but that requires your immediate attention. Usually, it made me upset or even fill me with anger that why does it always happen to me when I am doing such and such important thing. But when we reframe the problem, when we change the point of view from that **"I am the doer"** to **"Whatever My Mother will tell me to do, I will let her do through this body"**, we can start witnessing something magical start to happen. 

![[Pasted image 20241227094632.jpg]]

All the tension or irritation melts away or just don't even arise in the first place because, if something has come up then that is the will of the [[Divine Mother]]. Then who are we to get upset! Who am I to question her plan, because she is omniscient, omnipresent and omnipotent. I just let her do her job and do my part. This shifting in the frame of reference, is really unique and quite curious thing to watch, especially if you approached the problem like me.

---
When the will of the lord is visible in everything then how can it contradict with your own will. But, important is the fact that **we must do our part**, we must see **her presence in everything**, which we can easily do when we are praying for her constantly, call her name, the whole thing.
