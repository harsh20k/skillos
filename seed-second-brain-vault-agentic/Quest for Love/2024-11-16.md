# The Sword of Routine: Cutting Through the Chaos of Freedom
 
Being Saturday today, it’s one of those days usually meant for relaxation. But to actually relax we need to be really strict and I mean in a really martinet way, so that we have enough structure that keeps us from withering away as an individual. 

![](https://i.imgur.com/7o8eDNz.png)

 
Why does Premanandji say that “Treat your routine as [[God]]”? It is routine that takes away our general anxiety when we face a day full of 24 hours, and instead of looking at it as a basket of opportunities all we see is dreadful and ferocious chaos staring at us. No wonder, we feel paralysed just thinking about what should we do with our next 24 hours. 
 
There is no freedom without responsibilities, only children have that kind of luxury. Similarly, there is no organic progress without structuring the time and energy that we are given each and every day. How could we be thankful for such a great gift of a fresh new day, a brand new space and time to accomplish anything we wish for, if each second, minute and hour goes by thinking about the chaos such freedom brings. 
 
I feel a student who follows a certain time table accomplishes more than a hard working, 9 to 5 employee who is exhausted after each and every day. I think the inherent order in a student’s life plays a significant role in the general satisfaction levels. 
 
With freedom comes responsibility. Each new day brings with the freedom, mayhem, a war, a battle with our own base tendencies. The sword of routine can cut through all this disorder and we could probably be better than we were yesterday.
