# The Power of the Fallback Routine: Keeping Habits Alive Through Tough Times

Its a challenge to keep consistent habits during busy times or right times like some bodily illness.

It’s already a huge task to be consistent when healthy. But add on top of that the stress of disease and bodily daddies and agony and we have got ourselves a recipe for failed habits and broken steaks. 

![[Pasted image 20250310193131.jpg]]

Doing meditation and writing and coding and drawing daily seems to me a great identity to have. This identity  however entails that i perform these 4 core things daily! Each one of these are very cognitively taxing and takes a lot of effort and energy. 

What i found interesting and through my own experience that skipping one day just knocks you off. But if we maintain the momentum of habit through minimum viable tasks then we do not affect our long term growth and keep the fire going! 

So imagine one day like today when i just not have any energy. I got only 3 hours of sleep and also have flu like symptoms. What i could have is morning and evening 5 min meditations. And within my most productive hours i could have taken 5 minutes out for writing, 5 minutes for coding and 5 more minutes for drawing. All this merely takes around 25 minutes!! 

So next time i do not have that energy to follow my set routine i need to folkie this strategy of fallback routine for identity growth and masteryl.
