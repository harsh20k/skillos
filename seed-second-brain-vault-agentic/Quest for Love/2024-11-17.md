# True Knowledge: The Grace of Divine Realisation  
 
True knowledge is nothing less than everything combined. Knowledge could mean a number of things for different kinds of people. A carpenter would think constructing a table is knowledge because that lets his family earn a living. But what is true knowledge one might think?

![](https://i.imgur.com/cXGHY5t.png)


Knowledge of one thing could mean something for a person but that same knowledge might be considered trivial by the other. True knowledge is of very different nature. It is something knowing which there is nothing else to know. It is true for everybody, it is meaningful to everybody. And that is not earned by a person, instead it is given by the source of perception itself to the individual. 
 
That realisation is often considered by the average man steeped deeply in the mundanities of daily living and survival. He would question even the need for such an understanding. The deep roots of ego doesn’t let the man perceive the ephemerality of his body and mind. The very question of “What is the point of all this?” is the dawn of such realisation, and the source again being the heart of God. If God doesn’t grace us with this, we would keep living our lives seeking outward approvals and appreciations as the sole achievements of this short life, often met with an abrupt conclusion. 
 
Thus arises the need for the knowledge. The solution to our problems is not in earning more, eating better or living richer, but the removal of the root cause of ignorance.
