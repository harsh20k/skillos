# The Illusion of I: Desire, Habit, and the Path to Inner Freedom

We are all trying to be happy right? If the answer is yes then why does a drunkard drink? Isn't it obvious that he will only be happier once he quit drinking? 

If the answer is yes then why does a man masturbate or chew tobacco? Isn't it obvious that he will be happier once he quit these things. Man wants to be happy but is still doing the things that are stealing his happiness away. 

When God & [[Guru]] are the only source of happiness then why is man hell bent on doing the things exact opposite of what is prescribed? Why? Why doesn't God help those who start on this path and help them quit everything all at once?

![[Pasted image 20250501095457.jpg]]

The validity of this question can be pondered over a century and still no consensus be found by the worldly men. 

Let's say I want something. Now I do actually want that thing because there is I'ness associated with it. When someone is hungry and thinks completely believes that he is not hungry but his stomach, his body is hungry and needs nutrition then the hunger will not affect his mood or he won't become a hunger-monster.

When the body needs some chemical it is used to for a long time like tea, then the I'ness should not be associated with the need. Instead I should be distanced from the need of the body, then there is some sort of control now. 

Both persons i.e. the one who is completely identified with his hunger and then one who sees hunger as just a need of the body and not of the self, might look like eating a plate but the former might be completely drunk in enjoying the food and forget everything else, might eat more than the stomach needs, might eat bad things which might not even be healthy for his body who he is trying to appease.

But that I'ness completely derails the flow of events and the bondage is formed by the free soul into the different things of the world.
