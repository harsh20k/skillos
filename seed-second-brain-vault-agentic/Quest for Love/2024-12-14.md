# Grace Beyond the Veil

I did not meditate yesterday properly. The plain and simple reason of this lapse in [[Sadhana]] is the wrong judgement of putting [[God]] and [[Guru]] below the world in the priority list. 

![[Pasted image 20241214100300.jpg]]

If I ask myself this question if I love God. Then the answer will be the same as before and that would be affirmative. While, there could be all reasons to not be convinced to that contrary thought the true inclination to love God cannot be denied by the actions of the devotee. He can love God and still be a slave of the body and mind. It is however, imperative that the devotee asks for the ultimate guidance and deliverance from the same beloved. Nothing can change the fact that the love we feel for God is actually the love God feels for us and decides to reveal it in our hearts.

Because like any [[Truth]], the love of God is true. Its always there and will not fade when the earth and moon cease to exist. I am God's and God is mine is the maxim that will lead to final salvation. 

But there is also a point to note, that to actually experience God in the living flesh there should be complete surrender in thought, word and action. If any of them is not aligned with the ultimate nature of divine, either the [[Guru]] will take on the devotee's karma to facilitate his path towards the divine, or God will himself come and cleanse the devotee of all the ignorance so his true nature can shine forth and no veil appear between the two.

If this does not happen and the devotee reaches the deathbed then the grace of [[Guru]] will come in the form of divine memory and free the devotee at his final bidding. It is a win-win situation for the devotee who at no extra cost gets full discount for his place among the loving disciples of the [[God]].
