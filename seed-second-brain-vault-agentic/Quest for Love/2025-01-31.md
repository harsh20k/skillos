# Grace of the Guru’s Paduka: A Devotee’s Surrender
I think there must be a daily wake up routine because lately I have been experiencing the habit of masturbation generally assails me in the morning and I do feel guilty about it after doing that bad habit.

---
Today I heard about [[Guru]] kripa through devotion towards guru-paduka. When we have devotion for our beloved [[Gurudeva]] then all our actions become his seva and the love for God and Guru starts to bubble within us. 

When by devotional bhakti towards Guru's feet and his sandals we surrender ourselves to him completely and practice it everyday the love for god ascends inevitable no matter how bad karma anyone has. [[Premanandji]] said that if someone who steals money from others, who copulate with other's wives or someone who is violent, if he only take one rule of vrindavan vaas, then all his actions do not matter anymore and he becomes a sadhu and he will find God after the end of this life. 

---
![[Pasted image 20250131092020.jpg]]

---
God's love and devotion only shines in the heart when we feel devoted towards our [[Gurudeva]]'s form or mantra or image. Right after we inwardly accept to be reared by a true Guru in our heart all our karma is completely destroyed instantly and we will find ultimate peace and love guaranteed. 

The devotee who feels devotion to the guru will attract wealth, dhrama, desires and achieve moksha by the grace of the ultimate guru. Guru is none other than our ishta-deva. 

---
If we do not have devotion or we are not feeling it right now after feeling momentarily free in surrender to Guru, then also we have won the battle of life and death. All we have to do is try our best to come back to the mantra, centre our consciousness to our ishta and whenever possible try to feel the ultimate grace bestowed by the [[Guru]]. 

The power of devotion for [[Gurudeva]] is the most powerful practice. The ultimate culmination of all the practices is to feel that inward surrender to [[Guru]] and his words. Complete renunciation of the bodily worries and taking refuge in Guru's feet only is the end of all battles be it material or spiritual.

---
I am blessed to have had that feeling, to have been initiated by a true man of god and that gives me hope that maybe in this lifetime I will witness God and [[Divine Mother]] in her all glory with my eyes open and also try to see her everywhere my eyes go.
