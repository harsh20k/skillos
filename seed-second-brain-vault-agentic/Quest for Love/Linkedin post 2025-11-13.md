This first semester in CSCI 5308 at Dalhousie has been one of the most eye-opening parts of my studies so far.

Our professor, Dr. Tushar Sharma, designed the course to feel very close to real industry work. Instead of just doing regular assignments, we work in teams, meet with our “client” every week, present updates, and deal with changing goals. It feels(and is exactly same) like what real developers actually experience at work.

During our midterm presentation, I finally understood what our professor had said earlier i.e. **software engineering isn’t only about writing code, it’s also about people**.

Teamwork in real projects isn’t always perfect. Sometimes people contribute unevenly, tasks take longer than expected, and priorities keep changing. You have to learn how to communicate well, take initiative when needed, and stay professional even when things get tough. These are lessons you can’t just learn from slides or lectures.

From this course, I’ve realised that:

- **Planning** and **communication** are just as important as writing the best algorithm.
- Knowing what the **client needs** is more helpful than just showing them code.
- Projects **progress** when someone takes charge and keeps the team focused.
- **Good documentation** is key to keeping complex projects clear and consistent for everyone.

I’m so thankful for how this course is set up. It really gets us ready for what it’s like to work in the software industry. As a grad student , learning in this environment helps me develop not only my technical skills but also my mindset as a future professional.
