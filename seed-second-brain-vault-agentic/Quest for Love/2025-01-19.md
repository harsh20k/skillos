# The Uphill Path to Divinity

I have observed something really dangerous, a struggle of following the word of [[Gurudeva]]. When I decided to do energisation exercises I could not because that is the word of my beloved [[Gurudeva]] to whom I should be thankful for everything. 

![[Pasted image 20250119091158.jpg|center]]

Why can't I follow the Guru's word verbatim as it is? I then shifted to doing 10 min indoor runs every day in the morning before meditations. The answer to this question is the ego. The ego and I ness doesn't let the individual give completely themselves to someone. The ego prevents total surrender to [[God]] and [[Guru]]. All the various animalistic desires and fears are hiding in the seed form behind the bubble of ego.

---
This bubble of ego doesn't help the spiritual path at all, instead it creates a layer of butter or ghee so that the heart is insulated from the [[kripa]] of the [[Divine Mother]]. 

But what is interesting in this situation is the fact that we as individuals are not in complete control of our selves. We are merely just acting out the actions we programmed ourselves into our being. These subsystems as described by Dr. Jordan Peterson have a personality of their own and it is very hard to fight with them.

We are not capable of stopping a bad habit. This rogue personality is like a cancer that has grown rampant in our minds and taken root deep inside. Yet, there are some ways to facilitate this process of uprooting them. One is, to stop focussing on the removal or cessation of the bad habits but focus so immensely on the other objective or goal that the mere thought doing bad habit related actions does not arise. This is also corroborated by the general idea that the habits actually never go away but we can replace them with other good routines and habitual actions. 

---
Then coming back to ego, how do we put an end to this dangerous system so deep inside of our being that we don't even realise is separate from us. Complete renunciation from following the [[Man]]'s whims and wishes and following a structured routine is the first step in this process. By slowly ignoring its constant whims and desires we starve the [[Man]]'s supply and it start to become more pliable. The second step is to try to follow [[Guru]]'s word throughout our day as much as possible. This is what I am trying to accomplish but this being the hardest step, it can take some time but ultimately with [[Gurudeva]]'s grace and divine mercy we will succeed.

---
Soon leaving all the demands of [[Man]] we will be able to follow [[Guru]]'s words and even a slight deviation from their recommendation will feel painful and make us anxious.

Jai Guru! It is through Guru that we start following the [[Guru]]'s words, it is by his grace that we are able to walk this difficult uphill path to divinity and with his grace we will ultimately meet our [[Divine Mother]].
