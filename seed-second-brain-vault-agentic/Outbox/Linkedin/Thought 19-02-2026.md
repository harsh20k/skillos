Today I finally stopped treating “local-only” as a comfort zone and pushed a project to the cloud. 

Moving from `localhost:3000` to a real URL forced me to learn things tutorials often skip: environment variables, secrets, basic monitoring, and what actually happens when your app restarts or crashes.

I broke things, rolled back, fixed configs, and learned to read logs instead of just refreshing the browser and hoping.

It’s messy, a bit scary, and absolutely worth it.

What’s one lesson you learned the first time you deployed beyond your laptop?
