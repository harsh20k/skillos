Professor Lu encouraged me to check out Avanade's showcase. They're trying to expand their presence in Halifax.

Three senior people presented - Cornell Skyers (Director), Limysh Kalapala (Senior AI Engineer), and Mitul Shah (Senior Full Stack Developer).

Each shared their journey, Avanade's growth culture and technical challenges clients are facing today , what consulting actually looks like, and what matters most. Behavior first, technical second. And that did make sense since I also have a similar experience in consulting company Infosys. 

After the presentations, I got to talk with them.

Cornell the "city lead" is growing the Halifax team, has hiring approval. 

Limysh came up through an Avanade internship and shared some great insights. He said roles are blurring now. Data engineering, AI engineering - the lines aren't clear since this boom started.

Mitul also started as an intern, now works with major accounts like Disney Parks.

I shared my Cloud and Agent AI projects - CoinBaby and GroundSense. They responded well.

Thanks to Cornell, Limysh, and Mitul for the insights and encouragement.

What stuck with me most: Mitul said don't forget the fundamentals. Limysh said their clients run traditional Java, Spring Boot, Python stacks and they need engineers who can wire those systems into modern agentic AI pipelines. That's giving me ideas for what to build next.

Why do we think AI engineering means starting from scratch? The real work is integration with the new. Taking what already exists and making it intelligent.
