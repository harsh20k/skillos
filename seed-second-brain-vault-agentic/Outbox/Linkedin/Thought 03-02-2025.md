India's latest budget did something bold, a tax holiday until 2047 for foreign cloud providers who use Indian data centres to serve customers globally. Zero corporate tax on those profits for over twenty years.

India already has 132 data centres and 4 cloud providers. The tax holiday makes the next wave of builds a lot more attractive.

It will lead to:
- More hyperscaler investment in India
- India favoured over Singapore, Hong Kong, etc. for global capacity
- More competition and downward pressure on prices
- Higher demand for power and talent
- India more viable for global workloads without strict data residency
- Tradeoff: foregone tax revenue vs. jobs, investment, and infrastructure

India isn't playing the short game. It's betting that making it irresistible to build there will pay off—not in tax revenue today, but in becoming the world's digital infrastructure hub. The tradeoff is clear: foregone revenue now for long-term positioning.
