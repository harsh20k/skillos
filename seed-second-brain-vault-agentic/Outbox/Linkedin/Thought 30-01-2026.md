How did we even do cloud before Infrastructure as Code?

I've been thinking this how did cloud based development projects maintain themselves and "how did people not lose their minds?" 

AWS landed in 2006 
CloudFormation didn't show up until 2011
Terraform in 2014. 

In between: a lot of clicking. Console UIs. Manual provisioning. Guides that lived in some GOD tier Team member's head or a wiki that drifted out of date. Environments that were "probably the same" until they weren't.

A really interesting thought then is - we already had the same goal—repeatability, consistency but we just didn't have the tools yet. IaC didn't invent the need it gave us a language for it.

If you shipped to the cloud before Terraform or CloudFormation: what did your workflow actually look like?

## outline
- Hook: the question we forget to ask once a tool becomes normal
- Before IaC: timeline (AWS 2006, CloudFormation 2011, Terraform 2014); consoles, manual steps, tribal runbooks, environment drift
- Reframe: same human goal (repeatability), new primitives (code)
- CTA: invite stories from people who lived it
