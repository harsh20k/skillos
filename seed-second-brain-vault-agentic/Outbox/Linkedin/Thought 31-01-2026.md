A friend messaged me on LinkedIn: "Are you using AI to write these posts?"

I could have said yes or no. The honest answer is: I use a *framework*, and yes, LLM is a part of it.

Here's how it actually works.

I start with a short brief. Topic, length, goal—teach something, share a build, opinion, career update. Any keywords, any "avoid" rules. That's the constraint box. Without it, I'm just prompting into the void.

Then I have a style guide. A single doc that captures how I write—tone, rhythm, the kind of sentences I like, the voice. I extracted it from my own written text using LLM. So whatever gets drafted has to sound and "feel" like *me*, not like generic LinkedIn. 

The draft happens inside that box. I get a post that fits the brief and the style. I edit it. Sometimes a lot. The thinking is mine, the first pass is assisted.

Finally it goes into a file: Thought dd-mm-yyyy. Same structure every time. That way I can look back and see what I was trying to say.

So: am I using AI? Yes. Is AI "writing" my posts? No. It's a co-pilot. The framework—brief, style, edit, file—is what keeps it mine.

I heard an interesting perspective in an IG reel and it really shocked me,
"When anyone can create anything imaginable in an instant, then the most human element will not be novelty but rather that continuous human grit to keep showing up and FAIL REALLY FAST." 🤯🤯🤯🤯

Any artist can create a piece of "good music" but the greatest of the artists had a "HUGE BODY OF WORK". I think that is the differentiating factor.
