## title: Thought 06-03-2026
date: 06-03-2026
topic: "We won't search for movies, we'll prompt them" — abstraction of creation, prompt+edit, AI debate cycle
length: short

Saw a post that stuck: in a few years we won’t search for movies. We’ll prompt them.

It sounds obvious once you say it. We’re already abstracting the manual creative step into a single prompt. So it’s not a huge leap to imagine creators as people who prompt and edit. The craft becomes *what you ask for* and *what you keep*.

Then the usual cycle. “Will AI replace filmmakers?” Studios cut creative teams. They realise the mistake. They hire back. We’ve seen this pattern. 

The interesting question isn’t replacement. It’s who gets to sit at the prompt, and who gets to say what gets made.

## outline

- Hook: “We won’t search, we’ll prompt” — why it lands
- Abstraction of creation: from many steps to prompt + edit
- The cycle: debate → layoffs → regret → rehire (pattern we keep repeating)
- Reframe: not “replacement” but who controls the prompt and the green light
- CTA: what would you prompt for?
