Cursor has changed how I approach new codebases entirely.

Before, jumping into an unknown project meant hours of tracing functions and mapping dependencies. Now I get up to speed in minutes. Ask about the codebase, and Cursor explains the context and patterns.

But the real surprise? It's made me better at fixing my own mistakes.

Last week I found code I wrote one night - no comments, terrible names, functions that did everything. Instead of rewriting, I asked Cursor to help me understand what I was trying to do, then refactor it cleanly. An hour's work became 5 minutes.

The game changer? Connecting to my Obsidian notes vault via MCP server. Now I can research across all my notes while coding. It's like having my entire learning history as context!
