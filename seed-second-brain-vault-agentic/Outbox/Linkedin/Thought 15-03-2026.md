Yesterday I sat in a room full of students who are just starting (or restarting) their cloud journey.  

Dalhousie just launched its AWS Cloud Club — the first official AWS student community in Atlantic Canada — and honestly there was this mix of curiosity and real excitement in the room.  

Why does being around people like that feel so different?  
Because suddenly the abstract idea of “learning cloud” turns into faces, questions, and small first steps. It stops being a solo grind and starts feeling like a shared path.  

I’m especially grateful to YinNing (Chloe) Zhu, our AWS Cloud Captain and host for the evening, and to Professor Yang Lu, who shared many insights at the event and encouraged many of us to show up.

I don’t know where each of us will end up with AWS and cloud, but it’s encouraging to know we don’t have to walk it alone.
