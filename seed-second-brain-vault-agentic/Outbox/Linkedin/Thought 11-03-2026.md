Everyone is excited about agentic AI workflows right now.  
Very few people are asking a boring question:  
**What happens when you plug them into a messy codebase?**

Most teams imagine an army of smart agents fixing everything.  
In reality, agents mostly amplify whatever you already have:
- Clear architecture → faster iteration.  
- Stable contracts and events → reliable automation.  
- Spaghetti services and hidden side effects → expensive chaos.

An agent doesn’t “understand” your system the way a senior engineer does.  
It infers behaviour from code, logs, and docs.  
If those are inconsistent, incomplete, or tightly coupled, the agent spends more time fighting your design than shipping value.

Over the next few years, I think we’ll see a split:
- Teams treating agents as a bandage on bad systems will declare “AI doesn’t work here”.  
- Teams investing in clean domains, explicit boundaries, and good observability will quietly get a compounding advantage.

The bottleneck won’t be the next model release.  
It will be whether your system is simple enough for both humans and agents to reason about.

If you’re excited about agentic AI today, the uncomfortable question is:
**Would an agent survive a week inside your codebase?**
