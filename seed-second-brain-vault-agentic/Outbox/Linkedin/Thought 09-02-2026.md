A construction project manager told me something that stuck: if he saves the company $100k in a year, he gets a $50k bonus. Straightforward. Your savings, shared back.

It got me thinking: in software, do we see the same deal? You trim cloud spend, simplify deployments, kill unused instances—do companies ever tie that directly to your pay? Or is "cost savings" mostly a team or company metric, with no clear line to the person who did the work?

I don't have a clean answer. In construction, the link is obvious. In tech, it often feels invisible. Maybe that's the norm; maybe it's a gap.

Has your company ever rewarded you specifically for cutting infra or cloud costs—or is that idea basically absent where you work?
