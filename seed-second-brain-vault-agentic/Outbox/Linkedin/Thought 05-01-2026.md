Polling feels simple until you're checking a database 12,000 times per hour for updates that happen twice a day.

I learned this building a real-time notification system. Every 5 seconds, my service queried the database. Most queries returned nothing.

I switched to event-driven architecture. Instead of asking "has anything changed?", I listen for events. When something happens, I get notified. No wasted cycles.

But polling isn't always wrong. Few years back, I built an R-Shiny app visualizing air pollution data. A GCP service triggered hourly every 4 hours, collecting 6 data points across 13 locations every day. Polling made sense—predictable schedule, low frequency, no real-time need.

The tradeoff? Real-time needs events. Scheduled batch jobs can poll. The question isn't which is better—it's which fits the problem.

When did you last choose polling over events, and why?
