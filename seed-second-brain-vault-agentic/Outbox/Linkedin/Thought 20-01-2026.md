Cloud computing in 2026 isn't about the next big thing. It's about what we're actually doing with what we have.

Serverless isn't just cheaper but it forces us to design differently. Edge computing isn't a theory it's solving latency problems we couldn't before.

But here's what I think, the abstraction layers are getting deeper. We're building on platforms that abstract away infrastructure, and that changes how we think about systems. Is that good? I don't know. It makes things faster, but it also makes us more dependent on vendor ecosystems.

The trend isn't a new service. It's how we're learning to work within constraints we didn't choose.

## outline
- The shift from migration to architectural thinking
- Serverless as a design constraint, not just a cost optimization
- Edge computing solving real latency problems
- Deeper abstraction layers and vendor lock-in concerns
- The tension between speed and dependency
- Whether we're building resilience or just shifting complexity
