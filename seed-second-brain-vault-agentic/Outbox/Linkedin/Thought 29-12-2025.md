Why do we overcomplicate durability in distributed systems?

I've been thinking about write-ahead logs lately. Postgres has been doing this for decades - write the log first, then apply the change. That's it.

The beauty is in the simplicity. Before you modify data, you write what you're about to do. If the system crashes, you replay the log. No complex consensus algorithms needed. No distributed transactions. Just: log first, apply later.

This pattern scales to distributed databases because each node can maintain its own WAL. When you need replication, you share the log entries. The log becomes the source of truth, not the data itself.

We chase complex solutions when the answer is often the simplest one that works.
