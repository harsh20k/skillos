# Reflection on Marriage, Solitude, and Life's Uncertainties

It has almost been a month after my wedding and there have been a lot of updates that I need to go through today. First of all I would say that this life co-living with someone is not much different than the single life where I used to spend most of my time alone. However, this should not be misunderstood, that I feel lonely even after my marriage. 

![[Pasted image 20250301114843.jpg]]

I generally dislike the idea that spending time with anyone actually means spending time another person. Living a life with someone, does not in fact mean that we are living with someone else; our minds has a perception or an idea of the person next to us and we interact with that idea or ghost and based on our best judgement we try to fit in that idea with the already built understanding of our individual. 

This might seem complicated so let me try to simplify that. 

Let's say we are a child and have never seen an apple before but have eaten many other fruits. When we encounter this new round red coloured object our minds will race to find the category in which this new shiny object can fit in. By prodding and playing with it and after taking a small bite, we will find that it is juicy just like other fruits, although has a different texture, smells fruity and tastes sweet and "good" like the category "fruit". 

This makes any object feel familiar in our own preconceived mental map of the world. When we start living with a person, similarly start to categorise all these new ideas of a person into familiar categories so that we can make sense of this whole new idea of "Husband" or "Wife". 

---
You have to take all this with a grain of salt however, for all this is just my conjecture based on a few psychology books and lectures I have consumed. 🤷

### Delay in the visa process

I had my medical done for the Canada visa and they have found some opacities in my lungs so they are doing a follow up test that will give conclusive results after two months. 

This can be seen as a major setback. But as I always try to think, all setbacks are just opportunities for growth and reflection. 

This can push our plans to move to Canada from September this year to probably September of next year or worst case scenario we might have to drop this idea all together.

### We got our marriage certificate ✅

After a lot of effort and going to various lawyers and wasting a lot of time we got our marriage registered with the help of  [[EasyLegal]]. It cost us around 15000 but considering everything we had no other option.

If we had known that there would be delays due to medical we might not have spent more than 5-6 thousand on this which would have given our certificate in 1-2 months. 

### Finances

This is a tough one to describe, because there have been quite a few arguments that took place related to this. 

Since Suman is the sole-earner it has created some issues between us because of my lack of contribution to the family income, but we are trying to recover from this with a different mindset and understanding. 

This idea of the man being the major breadwinner is deeply engrained in all of us. When the wife or the woman takes that role it can be tough on the relationship. Suman has told her parents that my work is going fine, but since some time I have been struggling to see any major demand on my services offered on fiverr platform. 

Recently I started to give tuitions for earning some supplemental income but that too seems to be an uphill battle and I might have to abandon that in favour of spending time learning IOS applications and honing my artistic skills. 

We both are having major discussions due to all of this and how it might affect our relationship in the future. This also sparked a discussion that particularly was interesting on the **importance of productivity** contrasting the deep rooted idea of the **importance of earning money**. 

Since all of this is new territory we have to navigate this carefully and with at least some faith.

# In short..

Married life is nothing far too different than a solitary one I believe. 

There are problems in solitary living and there are problems in co-living with anyone. Both scenarios takes every ounce of strength we have in us. 

On the surface it might seem that married life comes with a different set of challenges but if we look closely all these are the same issues we all have been fighting within for eons.

Just like every house needs a metal rod that goes directly into the ground to take the shock of any lightning strike, any kind of life be it based on marriage or solitude needs a spiritual grounding to be of any advantage.
