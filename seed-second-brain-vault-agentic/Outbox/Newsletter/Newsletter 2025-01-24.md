# Embracing the Unknown- From Imposter to Infinite Possibilities

There have been some new developments from the last week since I shared. The biggest news has been that on 2nd February, in almost 10 days days I am getting married! 

As you can imagine we all involved have been only talking and thinking about the day of the wedding and what arrangements need to be made and other details.

Other than that on [[Inbox/QuickNotes/Suman]]'s suggestion I have started to explore the world of teaching and started taking python classes for a 8th standard student. It has only been two sessions, but I don't have any conclusive thoughts.

Initially I was only hesitant because of some self-doubts because of lack of "ANY" teaching experience in my life which I thought was a shame lol.

I am still not comfortable with the identify of a teacher and it feels kind of forced but I also know that that's what it feels to do anything unfamiliar or new. 

To feel like an imposter is totally natural and that is the same what I felt when I started making youtube videos or started doing animation. Actually after doing so many projects and years of experience it still feels odd to say it out loud that I am a freelance artist or an animator.

---
That feeling of denial that "No, I don't know anything different or unique" is very strong and I don't think it ever goes away. 

The interesting thing to note is that many people die without even experiencing this, because they have created an identity around what they are doing currently that if you take that away from them they are left with nothing!! 

Its then a blessing or a luxury that some people like us get to feel stupid and like an imposter posing like someone else we are not, because deep down we all know that no kind of work can define the person that we are. 

So I would like to welcome all the people who are just fed up with their daily grind trying to break the monotony of their lives by buying things or meeting new people or exploring different vistas. I would instead welcome them on a journey to a different identity anything other than the one they are holding so tightly. For once let's be someone else, however scary it might look like or how stupid we might look like.

Here a very apt like comes to mind said by Steve Jobs, "Stay Hungry, stay foolish."
